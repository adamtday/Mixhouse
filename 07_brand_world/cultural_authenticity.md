# Cultural Authenticity

## Purpose

State the hard constraints that keep MixHouse credible to the electronic-music scene, the talent, the audiences, the press, the labels, and the governance mechanisms that hold those constraints in place.

## Key Takeaways

- Cultural authenticity is a non-negotiable design constraint, not a marketing promise.
- It's protected by structural choices: advisor governance, judging panel selection, music rights deal, anti-pattern enforcement.
- The scene rejects formats it doesn't recognise. Recognition is earned through scene-native production, talent, and language.
- A single high-profile authenticity breach can end the franchise.

## Core Content

### What Authenticity Means Here

- **Talent credibility.** Cast, judges, mentors, and advisors are scene-native or scene-vouched.
- **Music integrity.** Tracks are released through real label structures with real splits.
- **Production literacy.** Crew know how the scene actually sounds, looks, and behaves.
- **Language fluency.** Copy and on-screen text use scene vocabulary correctly.
- **Cultural respect.** Sub-genres are represented by people from within them, not adjacent to them.

### Hard Constraints (Non-Negotiable)

1. **No villain edits.** Conflict is creative; not manufactured.
2. **No staged drama.** Real moments only.
3. **Original music only.** No covers, no contrived karaoke.
4. **Producers credited everywhere.** Songwriting, production, master.
5. **Mentors are credible.** No celebrity bookings without scene relevance.
6. **Sub-genre representation honest.** Afro house judged by people in afro house. Drum and bass judged by people in drum and bass.
7. **No fan-vote stunts.** Streaming and short-form proxies preserve format integrity.
8. **Talent rights deal transparent and competitive.** No rights traps.
9. **No on-screen mockery of music genres or fanbases.**
10. **No cultural appropriation in styling, casting, or programming.**

### Governance Mechanisms

| Mechanism | Function |
| --- | --- |
| **Scene Advisor Board** | Standing group reviews casting, judging, sub-genre representation. |
| **Resident Judges** | Scene-credible by selection; can flag breaches publicly. |
| **Mentor Drops** | Cross-section of scene voices; not vanity bookings. |
| **Music Supervision (central)** | Holds the line on releases, splits, label partners. |
| **Format Co. brand lead** | Reviews public-facing copy, social, and press for tone and language. |
| **Quarterly cultural review** | Independent scene-journalist consultation. |

### Press and Scene Press Relationships

- Pre-launch briefings with key scene press (Resident Advisor, Mixmag, DJ Mag, Pitchfork), substantive, not promotional.
- Open access to set / studios for scene press during pilot.
- Editorial respect: press are not gated from honest reporting.

### What Earns Scene Trust

| Move | Effect |
| --- | --- |
| Naming credible judges before launch | Casting funnel and applicant quality rises immediately. |
| Publishing the rights deal in plain language | Talent and scene press lean in. |
| Treating advisors as decision-makers, not figureheads | Word-of-mouth across the scene shifts. |
| Releasing pilot music through a real label structure | Press treats the show as substantive. |

### What Breaks Scene Trust (Permanent)

- Caricature edits of sub-genres or contestants.
- Reality-TV stereotypes mapped onto producers.
- Cover-song stunts.
- Rights deals that strip producers of long-tail income.
- Booking irrelevant celebrities as judges or mentors.

### Cross-Cultural Considerations

When MixHouse adapts internationally:

- Local advisor boards in each territory.
- Sub-genre representation localised (e.g. amapiano in South Africa, gqom in Lagos territory).
- Production crew with cultural fluency, not parachuted from a central operation.
- Music supervision central but with named local A&R.

## Objections Answered

| Objection | Response |
| --- | --- |
| "Cultural respect limits creative choices." | It deepens them. The format engine works because the scene believes in it. Limits here are competitive advantage, not constraint. |
| "What if a credible judge says something controversial?" | We choose judges with discernment, not for safety. Real opinions are valuable; bad-faith content is not. |
| "Authenticity is a marketing buzzword." | Yes, when claimed. Here it is structural: rights deal, advisor governance, judging selection. |
