# Anti-Patterns

## Purpose

State explicitly what MixHouse will not be, will not look like, will not sound like, and will not do, so contributors, partners, and agents can spot drift before it ships.

## Key Takeaways

- Anti-patterns are binding. If a proposed asset, copy line, or production choice maps to one of these patterns, it is rejected without further debate.
- The list covers brand, voice, music, cast, format, and commercial behaviour.
- The list will grow over time as new failure modes emerge, appended via Change Log.

## Core Content

### Brand and Visual Anti-Patterns

- Equaliser bars, waveforms, or audio-graphic decoration in graphic design.
- Neon-on-black "EDM party flyer" aesthetic.
- Smiley faces, glowsticks, generic festival imagery.
- Energy-drink colour palettes (acid greens, hot pinks on black).
- Heavy radial gradients, lens flares, or laser graphics.
- Aggressive condensed display fonts.
- Logo treatments with motion blur, glow, or wave distortion.
- Stock photography of generic DJs with hands in the air.

### Voice and Copy Anti-Patterns

See full banned-phrase list in `00_system/research_prompt_template.md`. Specifically for brand surfaces:

- Exclamation marks in long-form copy.
- "At the intersection of music, reality, and culture."
- "Where music meets [X]."
- "Get ready for…"
- "The future of music TV."
- "Revolutionary / disruptive / next-generation."
- "Authentic" used about ourselves.
- Marketing emojis in editorial copy.
- "We believe…" / "We're on a mission to…" framing.

### Music Anti-Patterns

- Cover-song challenges.
- "DJ battles" stunts that prioritise spectacle over craft.
- Re-using stock music libraries inside the format's own soundtrack.
- Releasing tracks without producer credit.
- Buyout rights structures.
- Token sub-genre representation without sub-genre-credible mentors / judges.

### Cast Anti-Patterns

- "Reality regulars" with no scene credentials.
- Auditionee-only contestants with no released material.
- Casting for stereotype rather than character.
- Casting that ignores geographic / sub-genre diversity.
- Mentor bookings driven by celebrity Q-score over scene credibility.

### Format Anti-Patterns

- Surprise twists that contradict the published mechanic.
- Direct phone-vote eliminations.
- Villain edits.
- Manufactured romance arcs.
- Live-audience boos / shaming as the show's emotional grammar.
- Overuse of voiceover narration ("Will Maya finish the track in time?!").

### Production Anti-Patterns

- Library music beds covering contestant productions.
- Excessive cutaway to "reaction shots" during performance scenes.
- TV-realistic sound design at `Drop Night` that overrides the venue's real acoustics.
- Crew without scene literacy at sensitive shooting locations.

### Commercial Anti-Patterns

- Sponsorship integrations as banner reads.
- Brand veto over judging or editorial decisions.
- Format-sale deals that fragment music rights across territories.
- Live-event partners with poor scene reputation.

### Press and Communications Anti-Patterns

- "Hot take" press tactics that depend on conflict cycles.
- Pre-canned soundbites in EPK that don't reflect the format.
- Press kits that exaggerate ratings expectations or talent commitments.
- Hidden disclosures (advertorial without disclosure).

### Anti-Patterns When Talking to the Scene

- Claiming we "discovered" sub-genres or artists.
- Framing the scene as something MixHouse "elevates".
- Using insider language as decoration.
- Pretending the show is by the scene when it is *with* the scene.

### Enforcement

- Every brand asset, copy review, casting decision, and production reel is checked against this list before sign-off.
- Owner of enforcement: Format Co. brand lead, with veto rights on any surface destined for public use.
- Repeat violations from a partner trigger contract review.

## Evidence / Sources

Not applicable. This is the codified brand discipline; its authority is internal.

## Objections Answered

| Objection | Response |
| --- | --- |
| "Some of these are too restrictive." | Restriction is the design constraint that makes the brand legible. We can move a constraint via Change Log, never silently. |
| "What if a brand partner insists on a banned pattern?" | They walk. The category exclusivity is sold on integration depth; banner reads aren't on offer. |
