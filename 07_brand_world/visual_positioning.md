# Visual Positioning

## Purpose

Define the visual identity of MixHouse, across show, website, marketing, social, and live, at the level of principles, not pixels. The design system in `09_design_system/` operationalises these principles.

## Key Takeaways

- MixHouse looks like prestige cinema, not television.
- Visual identity is location-led and motion-led; static brand assets exist but are not the brand.
- Type is editorial. Image is photographic. Motion is film. Layout is generous.
- The brand is recognisably *electronic music* without being literal (no equaliser bars, no neon stereotypes).

## Core Content

### Principles

1. **Cinematic before graphic.** Type, layout, and motion serve the imagery, not the other way around.
2. **Place is identity.** The residency, venues, and finale locations are the brand's primary visual vocabulary.
3. **Generous space.** Whitespace and slow pacing, the opposite of EDM-poster compression.
4. **Editorial type.** Serif headlines or technical sans, set with confidence. No "energy drink" display fonts.
5. **Real moments, real bodies.** Photography prioritises real performance / craft / atmosphere over staged composition.
6. **Sound is part of the visual.** Every primary surface has a sound layer; silence is also an option.

### Visual Anchors

| Surface | Visual anchor |
| --- | --- |
| Show open | Aerial residency wide → macro detail of gear → contestant face. |
| Episode | Five-act anatomy with cinematic restraint. |
| Website hero | One looping single-take video; one editorial line of type. |
| Social | Vertical clips with minimal supergraphics. |
| Live event | Cercle-tier ambition; venue + crowd + artist triangulated. |
| Investor deck | Editorial spreads; image-led; minimal copy per slide. |

### Type Posture

- One editorial display family. Possibly a slow, contemporary serif (e.g. modern grotesque/serif hybrid) or a precise technical sans.
- One supporting working sans.
- No script faces. No condensed display sans. No neon-glow effects.
- Type scale defined in `09_design_system/typography.md`.

### Colour Posture

- Anchored in **deep neutrals**: black, near-black, off-white, warm grey.
- Accent palette derived from the residency location (sand, ocean, neon-blue night, sunset orange, chosen per season).
- No fixed EDM-flavour neon palette.
- Detail in `09_design_system/color.md`.

### Photography & Cinematography Posture

- Photography is high-grain, high-contrast, location-rich.
- Cinematography references in `07_brand_world/moodboard_references.md`.
- Photo direction: real moments captured cinematically; no studio-lit press shots.

### Motion Posture

- Motion is film-like: slow camera moves, considered cuts, real audio.
- No flash transitions, no kinetic typography for its own sake.
- Detail in `09_design_system/motion.md`.

### Visual Anti-Patterns

See full list in `07_brand_world/anti_patterns.md`. Specifically forbidden visually:

- Equaliser bars / waveform decoration.
- Stereotyped DJ silhouettes.
- Glowsticks, smiley faces, generic festival imagery.
- Brand colours pulled from energy-drink palettes.
- Heavy-handed gradient lighting effects.

### Logo (TBD)

- Final logo design not in scope of this file.
- Working principle: simple wordmark, editorial spirit, monospaced or technical sans accent.
- Final lockup to live in `09_design_system/tokens.md` once approved.

### Visual Identity Tested Against

- Would a Cercle viewer recognise this as kin to Cercle in ambition?
- Would a *Drive to Survive* viewer recognise this as prestige unscripted?
- Would a Berghain regular recognise this as scene-respectful?
- Would a brand partner see this as a premium category?

All four must answer yes.

## Objections Answered

| Objection | Response |
| --- | --- |
| "Restraint may read as boring to a casual viewer." | Restraint reads as confidence at the prestige tier. Casual viewers respond to atmosphere, not noise. |
| "Brand identity needs to be 'fun' for music TV." | Fun is in the music, the cast, and the venues. The brand frame is editorial; the content is alive. |
