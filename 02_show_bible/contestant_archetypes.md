# Contestant Archetypes

## Purpose

Provide a working taxonomy of contestant archetypes so casting can build a balanced ensemble that produces the character chemistry, conflict, and craft diversity the format requires.

## Key Takeaways

- A balanced cast has at least one of each archetype below, and never two of the same dominant type.
- Casting is for character first, craft second, scene credibility third — and every contestant must clear all three bars.
- "Reality stereotypes" are explicitly off-limits. Diversity here is genre, region, sound, and personality, not contrived characters.

## Core Content

### Core Archetypes

| Archetype | Defining trait | Story they bring | Risk to manage |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **The Prodigy** | Technical mastery beyond their years. | "Can craft alone win?" arc. | Can read cold on camera. |
| **The Star** | Magnetic performer / DJ instinct. | "Can charisma cover for craft?" arc. | May coast on stage presence. |
| **The Crate-Digger** | Encyclopaedic genre knowledge. | "Educator" arc; bridges scenes. | Can read elitist. |
| **The Outsider** | From a non-traditional scene / region. | "Whole new sound" arc. | Risk of being typecast. |
| **The Veteran** | Older / has been close to a break. | Stakes arc; second-chance energy. | Risk of cynicism on camera. |
| **The Producer's Producer** | Lives in the studio; quiet. | Craft arc; quiet-hero edit. | Limited spoken-to-camera content. |
| **The Hyphenate** | DJ + visual artist + label runner. | "Builds a world" arc. | Can over-extend creatively in-show. |
| **The Wildcard** | Genre-blender, rule-breaker. | High variance; defining moments. | Inconsistent output. |

### Cast Composition Heuristics

For a cast of 12–16:
- Cover all eight archetypes above.
- Include 2–3 duos / collaborator pairs.
- Geographic and stylistic diversity (representing 4+ regional scenes).
- Gender, ethnicity, and sexuality balance reflecting the scene's actual demographics, not a manufactured ratio.
- Age range broadly 19–34, with one Veteran above 30.

### Casting Filters

Every contestant must clear three bars:

1. **Character** — magnetic on camera; can carry a confessional scene; has a real opinion.
2. **Craft** — can finish a releasable track to scene-credible quality.
3. **Scene credibility** — has played a real gig, released real music, or been validated by named scene actors.

Failing any one disqualifies. We will not cast "characters" who can't produce, "producers" who can't carry a scene, or "TV-ready" applicants with no scene grounding.

### What We Will Not Cast

- Pure auditionees with no released or gigging history.
- "Reality regulars" with a multi-show cv.
- Contestants who openly want fame more than music.
- Anyone whose public history conflicts with the show's `cultural_authenticity` principles (see `07_brand_world/cultural_authenticity.md`).

### Character Briefs (for casting outreach)

#### The Prodigy
> "You're 21, you've been making music since 13, and your tracks are technically further on than your social numbers suggest. You've never had a stage. We'll give you one."

#### The Star
> "You walk into a club and the energy turns. You're a DJ before you're a producer, but you want to be both. We'll teach you to finish what you start."

#### The Outsider
> "Your scene is two cities and an internet community. Nobody in the global mainstream knows it yet. We'll bring it to a global audience — and you with it."

These briefs are direct sources for casting calls (`06_production_strategy/casting_pipeline.md`).

## Objections Answered

| Objection | Response |
| --- | --- |
| "Archetypes flatten people." | Archetypes are casting *filters*, not character writing. Each contestant is fully themselves on screen. |
| "What if our best applicant is a duplicate archetype?" | Cast the best people. Archetypes ensure breadth; they don't override quality. |
