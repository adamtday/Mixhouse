# Show Premise

## Purpose

State the show premise at the level needed for a one-page pitch: hook, world, stakes, conflict, characters, and seasonal payoff. This is the file every external collaborator reads first about MixHouse.

## Key Takeaways

- A residency of emerging electronic producers lives together and creates finished, releasable tracks on a recurring cadence.
- Each episode pays off with a live `Drop Night`: a curated club event where the week's tracks are debuted and reacted to in real time.
- Eliminations combine expert judging, live-audience reaction, and post-release streaming performance, a multi-source `Audience Index`.
- **Proposed.** The season finale culminates in a headline `Final Set` at a tentpole festival, with a defined record-release outcome and touring package for the winner. Specific partners, deal shapes, and venues are not yet committed.
- Cinematic execution and character residency are the difference between MixHouse and prior DJ formats that failed.

## Core Content

### Logline (Working Draft)

> A residency of emerging electronic-music producers lives, creates, and competes to release the records, the sets, and the careers that will define a season, judged by the scene that made them, the crowd that books them, and the streams that prove it.

The earlier draft of this logline used "define a generation." Removed pending substantive review: ambition larger than the show can deliver pre-pilot is a credibility cost we choose not to pay. The line remains draft until copy review.

### World

The show is set in a single, deliberately chosen residency location for the season. The location is a character: a beach in Ibiza, a warehouse in Berlin, a desert compound, a rooftop in Mexico City. Production design merges the lived-in intimacy of a Love Island villa with the architectural ambition of a Cercle livestream venue.

Inside the residency are:
- Living quarters built for filming.
- Multiple production studios with industry-grade gear.
- A flexible performance space rigged for nightly live recording.
- A control room that doubles as the editing/A&R hub.

Outside the residency, episode arcs take contestants to local clubs, mentor sessions, label meetings, and, in finale weeks, global festival stages.

### Stakes

- **Episode stakes:** the week's track must be finished, mastered, and ready for `Drop Night`. Failure is visible.
- **Season stakes (proposed).** The winner receives a defined record-release outcome, a `Final Set` at a flagship festival, and a touring package. Specific terms are proposed at this stage, not committed.
- **Scene stakes:** contestants are accountable to scene-credible judges and audiences. The competition is reputational, not just financial.

### Characters

- **Contestants**: typically 12–16 emerging producers/DJs cast for craft, character, and scene credibility. Archetypes in `02_show_bible/contestant_archetypes.md`.
- **Resident judges**: 2–3 scene-credible artists / A&R / promoters whose feedback is weighted in elimination.
- **Mentor drops**: recurring guest appearances by established artists; not panel judges, but creative collaborators.
- **The Audience**: both the live `Drop Night` crowd and the streaming audience act as a third character whose reaction matters.

### Conflict

Conflict is creative and competitive, not contrived. No villain edits. No fake romances. The drama is:
- Can the track be finished by Friday?
- Will the crowd react?
- Will the streams convert?
- Whose voice as a producer is emerging, and whose is being lost?

### Seasonal Payoff (Proposed)

By the finale, the format intends to deliver:
- A small group of contestants with measurable streaming followings.
- A season soundtrack released as a real commercial product.
- A festival-anchored finale and a defined release schedule for the winner.
- An opened second-season casting funnel.

These are the intended outcomes. Specific volumes, partners, and timings are working assumptions and will be calibrated against pilot performance and confirmed partnerships.

### What This Premise Is Not

- Not a singing show.
- Not a karaoke / cover-song format.
- Not a documentary series about existing famous DJs.
- Not a game show with electronic-music aesthetic.
- Not a behind-the-scenes festival show.

### One-Paragraph Pitch (Working Draft)

MixHouse is a cinematic competition franchise about the producers and DJs making the music a season is built around. A residency of emerging electronic artists lives and creates together, finishing new tracks on a recurring cadence and debuting them at `Drop Night`: a curated club event where the scene reacts in real time. Eliminations are decided by expert judges, live crowd response, and streaming performance, layered through one of the most ambitious music release pipelines ever proposed for an unscripted format. The season is built to culminate in a `Final Set` at a flagship festival, with a defined record-release outcome for the winner and a season soundtrack released as a real commercial product.

(Working draft. The previous version referenced music that "defines a generation" and "a label deal for the winner" as if committed. Both have been softened pending copy review and partnership confirmation.)
