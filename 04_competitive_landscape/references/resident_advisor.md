# Resident Advisor (Cluster Analysis)

## Purpose

Deep cluster analysis of Resident Advisor (RA) as the single most important scene-credible-electronic publication reference in the KB. Four surfaces analysed: homepage, magazine, reviews, podcast. RA is the scene-recognition benchmark for any MixHouse music-facing editorial surface; this file extracts the mechanisms that have sustained RA's editorial credibility across two decades of platform shifts.

## Key Takeaways

- RA's editorial credibility is built on four reinforcing decisions: assumed-knowledge editorial voice, location-as-first-class entity, parallel-pillar information architecture, and archive-as-asset thinking.
- The publication refuses several patterns that MixHouse should also refuse: aspirational marketing copy, generic genre labels, single-platform thinking.
- For MixHouse, RA is the single most important reference for music-facing copy, sub-genre vocabulary, and the editorial discipline of trusting audiences to know the scene.
- RA's brand world is operationally minimalist (clean sans-serif, restrained hierarchy, card-based content density) but its editorial register is rich. The lesson is that editorial richness does not require visual richness.
- For the cast-and-residency adjacency, RA's per-city event listings and RA Picks curation are useful patterns for MixHouse residency-and-finale geography.

### What Works (Cross-Surface Mechanisms)

#### Mechanism 1: Assumed-Knowledge Editorial Voice

Across all four surfaces, RA writes for the scene. Scene vocabulary ("footwork sound", "baile funk", "IDM icons", "808s", "big-room trance") appears without explanation. Outsiders are welcome but not catered to. The editorial voice signals that the publication is by and for people who already know.

This is the single most translatable RA mechanism for MixHouse music-facing copy. Outsider-catering language ("electronic dance music" instead of "electronic", explanation of who Carl Cox is) signals to scene audiences that the publication is not theirs. The discipline is to trust the audience.

#### Mechanism 2: Location-As-First-Class Entity

RA's homepage carries a regional selector and city-specific event listings. The city is the editorial unit. Magazine features regularly profile cities ("Bali's Temple of Sound", "Tokyo wax specialist"). The publication treats scene geography as first-class content.

For MixHouse, this transfers to the residency-and-finale geography. The cities MixHouse operates in are first-class editorial subjects: Ibiza, Berlin, Mexico City, wherever the cycles land. The cities have history, characters, sub-scenes; the brand world surfaces them.

#### Mechanism 3: Parallel-Pillar Information Architecture

RA holds together five parallel content pillars (Events, Music, Magazine, Reviews, Podcast) without one dominating. The home page lets readers self-route. The pillars do not compete; each serves a specific scene need.

For MixHouse, this maps to the show / world / audience / business-model parallel-surface architecture. No single surface dominates; each serves a specific audience need. Readers self-route from the home page.

#### Mechanism 4: Archive-As-Asset Thinking

The RA Podcast section surfaces a deep chronological archive as discoverable rather than hiding it. The numbered RA.#### sequence accumulates editorial weight. Old episodes remain valuable; the archive is part of the publication's value.

For MixHouse, archive-as-asset is the working pattern for cycle archives once multiple cycles accumulate. Old cycles do not become irrelevant; they become deeper context. The site architecture should support archive depth as discoverability, not hide it.

### What Fails

- **The card-grid density at homepage scale fails on mobile.** RA's home page is reading-rhythm-rich on desktop and dense on mobile; the discipline does not transfer wholesale to MixHouse public surfaces that lead with cinematic imagery.
- **The B-Corp footer badge is brand-world clutter.** Restraint discipline rejects badges and certifications in the footer; the B-Corp signal is meaningful in publishing-industry contexts but reads as decoration on the visual layer.
- **The regional-gating pattern is operationally complex.** RA's location-specific home page requires location detection, regional content management, and audience-segmentation logic. MixHouse is single-region per cycle; the operational pattern does not transfer.

### Pacing Observations

RA's editorial pacing is reading-rich, not scroll-rich:

- Home page surfaces high content density above the fold; readers scan rather than scroll-to-discover.
- Magazine articles use long-form reading rhythm; spreads are designed for reading, not for scrolling.
- Podcast and reviews use card-grid scanning rhythm for archive depth.

MixHouse public surfaces should use a different baseline pacing (scroll-rich, cinematic-restraint) but can adopt RA's reading-rich pacing for specific depth surfaces (mentor profile pages, residency city pages, cycle archive pages).

### Interaction Observations

- Year-and-month navigation (Podcast): Previous / Next buttons for chronological browsing.
- Tab switching (Reviews): RA Recommends / Albums / Singles tab states preserved on scroll.
- Card click (everywhere): standard editorial card click pattern with thumbnail-and-meta.
- Search (top nav): standard publication search.

No motion-heavy interaction patterns. No scroll-jacking. No modal interruption. The discipline is editorial.

### Typography Observations

- Single sans-serif family across all surfaces. Hierarchy through scale and weight.
- Headlines in moderate display weight; bylines and metadata in smaller weight with reduced opacity.
- Tag labels (genre tags, category badges) styled as "Circle" badges with restraint.
- Body type maintains generous line-height in long-form features.

The type discipline mirrors Apple's: single-family hierarchy. The difference is voice register, not type system.

### Motion Observations

Minimal. RA's site is functionally static. Scroll behaviour is standard. No parallax, no auto-play, no transition density. The publication trusts its content to carry; motion would distract.

This is a useful reference for MixHouse depth surfaces (mentor profile pages, cycle archive pages) where the content should carry rather than the motion.

### Audience Trust Mechanism

RA earns trust through four compounding decisions:

1. **Specific scene vocabulary used without explanation.** Readers verify the publication's expertise by recognising the vocabulary; the recognition is the trust signal.
2. **Reviewer bylines visible at card level.** Audiences form opinions about reviewers; the visibility makes the editorial accountable.
3. **Per-city event listings tied to RA Picks.** The publication's curation is auditable: scene insiders can verify whether RA Picks are scene-credible.
4. **Long archive depth as discoverability.** The archive's depth is the publication's history; the depth is itself credibility.

For MixHouse, all four mechanisms translate to music-facing surfaces. The cinematography credibility lives in `04_competitive_landscape/cinematography_language.md`; the editorial credibility lives here.

### What Should Translate To MixHouse

- Assumed-knowledge editorial voice for all music-facing copy. Trust the audience.
- Location-as-first-class entity for residency-and-finale geography. Cities are editorial subjects.
- Parallel-pillar information architecture for show / world / audience / business-model surfaces. Readers self-route.
- Archive-as-asset thinking for cycle archives. Depth is discoverability.
- Reviewer-byline-visibility for any opinionated editorial content.
- Sub-genre-tag vocabulary discipline (specific tags, never generic labels).

### What Should NOT Translate To MixHouse

- The card-grid information density at homepage scale (MixHouse leads with cinematic restraint).
- The eight-platform social-footer pattern.
- The location-gated regional selector pattern.
- The B-Corp footer-badge pattern.
- The reviews-and-podcast pillar architecture (those are RA's commercial pillars; MixHouse has different commercial pillars).

### What Should Be Reinterpreted

- RA's per-city event listings rework for MixHouse as per-city residency hubs.
- RA's RA Picks curation reworks for MixHouse as season-curation editorial content.
- RA's chronological podcast archive reworks for MixHouse as season-by-season cycle archive.
- RA's reviewer bylines rework for MixHouse as judge / mentor critique attribution.
