# The Voice

## Purpose

Identify what `The Voice` proves about music-led franchise economics, where its format limits are, and which lessons MixHouse should adopt or deliberately reject.

## Key Takeaways

- `The Voice` is the canonical music-franchise success story for format sales and territory adaptation.
- Its core format is a singing audition with rotating-chair mechanic; the music it produces is overwhelmingly cover-led, not original.
- Its long-term winner economics are weak — winners rarely sustain careers.
- The format-sale playbook (territory adaptation, local judges) is directly applicable to MixHouse; the audition-and-cover model is not.

## Core Content

### What It Proves

- Music formats can sustain multi-decade global runs.
- Local-judge adaptation works as a territory-scaling mechanism.
- Live-format theatre (chair turns, battles) drives appointment viewing.
- Strong brand recognition supports merchandising and live-event extensions.

### What MixHouse Adopts

- Format-sale playbook: clearly transportable rules, swap-in local judges, residency localised.
- Visible mechanics: the elimination math and the `Drop Night` payoff are designed to be on-screen and unambiguous.
- Star-judge cycling between seasons as a content engine.

### What MixHouse Rejects

- Covers as the unit of music. MixHouse releases originals.
- Single-vertical format (singing performance only). MixHouse uses residency + studio + performance.
- Weak post-show artist economics. MixHouse runs the release pipeline through real labels and partners.
- Audition-driven storytelling. MixHouse pre-casts known scene producers.

### Lessons for the Franchise Stack

- Format sales pay for years after the flagship hits.
- Multi-territory IP gives sponsorship category leverage.
- Star-judge churn keeps the format renewable but is fragile.

### Where We're Stronger

| Dimension | `The Voice` | MixHouse |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Music as product | Covers, ephemeral | Originals, released |
| Audience age | 35–55+ heavy | 18–34 heavy |
| Scene credibility | Low among genre purists | Designed-in |
| Streamer demo fit | Weak | Strong |

### Where We're Weaker (today)

- Brand recognition (zero vs global).
- Hard mechanic on screen (we trade simplicity for depth).
- Star judges currently absent (advisor list TBD).
