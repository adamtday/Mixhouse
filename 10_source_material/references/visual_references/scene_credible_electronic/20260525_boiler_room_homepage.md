# Boiler Room Homepage

## Observed Page Content

Modular carousel-based layout with horizontal scrolling sections: Upcoming Events, Shop, Cities, New Content. Primary navigation top-left (Events, Video, Audio, Shop, Editorial, Broadcast Lab). Secondary footer with project links, legal information, social media.

Content categories:
- Events: ticketed live sessions across global cities (Nottingham, NYC, London, LA, DC observed)
- Archive: tagged recordings with genre labels (Drum & Bass, Techno, Grime, Afro House)
- Merchandise: apparel and accessories ($30 to $340 range)
- Cities: localised content hubs (12+ major cities)
- Projects: specialised initiatives (True Music, Broadcast Lab, SYSTEM)

Minimal sans-serif type system. Genre tags function as metadata vocabulary, establishing credibility through specificity. Design emphasises photography and video thumbnails over textual narrative.

Sample editorial register:
- "the sounds shaping the scene right now"
- "a relentless showcase of bass, grime, jungle + beyond"

Tone: conversational yet authoritative; insider cultural authority with accessible enthusiasm.

## Mechanism Analysis

Boiler Room operates a different scene-credibility mechanism from Cercle or RA:

1. **Archive-meets-prestige-event-curator framing.** The platform balances three identities (event promoter, video archive, lifestyle brand) without picking one as dominant. The home page lets each surface.
2. **Genre-tag vocabulary as credibility currency.** Specific sub-genre tagging (Drum & Bass, Grime, Afro House) signals editorial competence; the absence of generic labels ("EDM", "club music") signals scene fluency.
3. **City-localised content hubs.** Place-based content architecture acknowledges that the scene is geographically distributed and culturally place-specific.

## What Should Translate To MixHouse

- Sub-genre-tag-vocabulary discipline for any MixHouse music-categorisation surface. Specific tags signal scene fluency; generic labels signal outsider perspective.
- City-localised content hub pattern for the cycles-and-finales geography. Each residency city earns its own dedicated content hub.
- Multi-identity framing as a category: MixHouse is media, residency programme, festival, and label simultaneously; the home page should allow each to surface.

## What Should NOT Translate To MixHouse

- The horizontal-scrolling carousel pattern at homepage scale (MixHouse home page leads with vertical narrative scroll).
- The merchandise-as-primary-section pattern.
- The events-ticketing-as-primary-entry pattern (MixHouse is not a ticketing platform; events appear in context, not as primary navigation).
- The Broadcast Lab / SYSTEM / True Music project-naming pattern (these are Boiler Room signatures; adopting the pattern is pastiche).
