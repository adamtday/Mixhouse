# Resident Advisor Magazine Section

## Observed Page Content

Modular vertical sections: Latest News, Popular News, Latest Features, Film, RA Exchange, Feature Series. Hierarchical navigation: primary menu items (Events, Music, Magazine) lead to secondary categories.

No traditional hero image dominates. Design prioritises content density; consistent card-based modules with thumbnail imagery. Magazine section announced simply as "Magazine" with descriptor "Interviews, films and news from across the scene."

Clean modernist sans-serif type system. Headlines use sans-serif weights to distinguish article titles from metadata (dates, categories). Category tags styled as "Circle" badges signal content taxonomy. Restraint suggests intellectual, design-forward editorial voice prioritising legibility over visual spectacle.

Feature content organised by series: "The Art Of DJing", "Best Music", "Top Ten Festivals", "Breaking Through". News stories tagged by category (Festival, Magazine, Mix of the day).

Sample headlines:
- "Klymax Discotheque: Bali's Temple of Sound"
- "Electronic Music's Most Memorable Launches: Blimps, DJ booking stunts, art installation disasters"

## Mechanism Analysis

The magazine section reveals RA's editorial discipline at depth:

1. **No-hero-image discipline.** The magazine refuses the home-page-hero pattern. Content density signals seriousness; visual spectacle would undercut the editorial register.
2. **Feature-series architecture.** Recurring series ("The Art Of DJing", "Best Music") create reader return-paths and editorial coherence across articles. Audiences develop relationships with the series.
3. **Headline craft.** Titles communicate both subject and angle. "Bali's Temple of Sound" is editorial framing; "Most Memorable Launches" with the parenthetical "(Blimps, DJ booking stunts, art installation disasters)" is voice and specificity.

## What Should Translate To MixHouse

- Feature-series architecture for any MixHouse companion editorial surface: recurring framings give audiences a reason to return.
- Headline-craft discipline: titles communicate both subject and angle, never one without the other.
- No-hero-image discipline for depth-content surfaces where density carries the page rather than imagery.

## What Should NOT Translate To MixHouse

- The card-grid information density at homepage scale.
- The category-tag-as-circle-badge visual treatment (works for RA's design system; MixHouse type discipline does not use badges).
- The reviews-and-podcast pillar architecture (those are RA's commercial pillars; MixHouse has different commercial pillars).
