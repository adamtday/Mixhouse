# Resident Advisor Podcast Section

## Observed Page Content

Dedicated podcast section anchored by global navigation (Events, Music, Magazine). Chronological grid layout: featured weekly episode at top, archived episodes organised by month and year.

Clean sans-serif system with prominent episode titles (h3), smaller descriptive subtitles, metadata (episode numbers, dates). Artist names emphasised in headers. Breadcrumb-and-arrow navigation for directional flow.

Episodes organised by year and month (May through January for 2026 at capture). Year navigation via Previous / Next buttons. Search functionality in top nav.

Informal, enthusiastic editorial voice with brief descriptive lines per mix:
- "The São Paulo-based artist tears through baile funk, ballroom, kuduro and techno in a turbo-charged showcase of global club music's cutting edge"
- "90 minutes of blissful, sun-soaked house from the essential UK producer duo"

Podcast type: artist-curated mix series featuring established and emerging electronic / dance music producers worldwide. Each installment is a branded mix (RA.####) with accompanying artist commentary.

Footer: corporate links (About, Advertise, Jobs), app promotions, social media (YouTube, SoundCloud, Spotify, Instagram, X), legal / privacy documents, B Corp certification noted.

## Mechanism Analysis

The podcast hub demonstrates archive-as-asset thinking:

1. **Chronological-archive-as-feature.** The deep archive is itself part of the editorial value; year-and-month navigation surfaces archive depth as discoverable rather than hiding it.
2. **Branded numbered mix series.** RA.#### sequential numbering accumulates editorial weight over time. The series becomes a fixture audiences track.
3. **Per-mix editorial copy as access point.** Short descriptive lines per mix do discovery work. Audiences scan the lines to choose what to listen to.

## What Should Translate To MixHouse

- Chronological-archive-as-feature pattern for MixHouse cycle archives once multiple cycles or seasons accumulate. The depth becomes discoverability.
- Branded sequential mix series concept: a MixHouse-branded mix series featuring residents, judges, and alumni becomes a brand-world fixture beyond the show itself.
- Per-mix editorial copy as a discovery tool.

## What Should NOT Translate To MixHouse

- The B-Corp footer-badge pattern (MixHouse footer carries editorial restraint, not certification badges).
- The five-platform social listing pattern.
- The chronological-only navigation as the sole archive access pattern (MixHouse archive will warrant additional access patterns: by producer, by venue, by sub-genre).
