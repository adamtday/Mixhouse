# Cercle Homepage

## Observed Page Content

Modular card-based layout with the Cercle logo centred at the top. Hero section: full-width image of a performer at a screen, establishing visual brand identity immediately.

Six primary navigation sections presented as distinct cards with accompanying imagery:
- Cercle ODYSSEY (immersive musical journeys)
- Cercle Festival (large-scale outdoor events)
- Cercle MOMENT (intimate experiences)
- Cercle RECORDS (music label operations)
- Cercle SHOP (merchandise retail)
- Newsletter signup (community engagement)

Each card includes a right-arrow icon, maintaining consistent interaction patterns.

Photography on cards: hot air balloons over Cappadocia, crowded festival grounds, Earth from space. Premium, globe-spanning aesthetic. Typography appears minimal and modern.

Brand positioning statement (visible on the homepage): "Cercle is an artistic movement that hosts and curates experiences, events, festivals, a record label, and immersive musical journeys."

Eight social / streaming icons anchor the footer (Instagram, YouTube, TikTok, Facebook, LinkedIn, Spotify, Deezer, Apple Music) plus a contact email. The homepage avoids traditional artist / location / year filtering, instead emphasising experience type as the primary taxonomy.

## Mechanism Analysis

This is a significant update to the Cercle reference. The mechanism:

1. **Experience-type taxonomy as primary navigation.** Cercle has moved past the single-livestream-format identity into a multi-property brand. The home page surfaces the experience-type taxonomy (ODYSSEY, Festival, MOMENT, RECORDS, SHOP), not the artist or location archive.
2. **Brand positioning as artistic movement, not as event series.** The "artistic movement" framing claims cultural authority beyond commercial event production. The single sentence is the strategic claim.
3. **Single-image-per-card brand register.** Each navigation card carries one defining image. No card-density information cramming; each card is a brand-world statement.

## What Should Translate To MixHouse

- Experience-type taxonomy as a primary navigation pattern when MixHouse brand expands beyond the show into adjacent properties (residency programmes, label, festival activation, archive).
- Single-defining-image-per-card brand register for any navigation card system.
- Brand-positioning-as-claim pattern: a single short sentence carrying the brand's strategic claim, not a marketing tagline.
- "Artistic movement" register as a position to consider for MixHouse positioning copy (not adopt verbatim, but consider the register).

## What Should NOT Translate To MixHouse

- The eight-platform social-footer pattern (MixHouse footer is editorial-restrained).
- The shop-as-primary-navigation card pattern (MixHouse merchandise, if any, is a secondary commerce surface, not a primary navigation entry).
- The all-caps property naming convention (ODYSSEY, MOMENT, RECORDS) is a Cercle brand signature; adopting it would be pastiche.
