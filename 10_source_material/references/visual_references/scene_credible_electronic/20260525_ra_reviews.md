# Resident Advisor Reviews Section

## Observed Page Content

Music reviews hub: album, single, EP critiques. Reviews organised into three parallel taxonomies: "RA Recommends" (editorial picks), "Albums", "Singles". Each category displays identical content structures with cross-promotion of standout reviews.

Information architecture: breadcrumb pathway (Music > Reviews), three tabbed sections with independent "View more" gateways, standardised review cards (record label, artist-title, descriptive summary, reviewer byline).

Minimalist sans-serif type system with restrained hierarchy. Editorial copy signals expertise through technical specificity: references to "IDM icons", "808s", "big-room trance" assume reader familiarity. Descriptive language ranges from poetic ("aqueous club jams") to visceral ("Ice-cold").

Sample editorial lines:
- "Warp's IDM icons resurface after 13 years with a dark, disquieting record shaped by a violent world."
- "One of last year's best albums is recast into a set of aqueous club jams."

## Mechanism Analysis

The reviews section demonstrates RA's critical voice:

1. **Critical writing as scene service.** Reviews assume readers want to know what is worth their time. The writing is opinionated, specific, and short.
2. **Breadcrumb-and-tab architecture for depth.** Music > Reviews routing tells readers where they are; tabs let them filter without leaving the page.
3. **Reviewer byline visible at the card level.** Bylines carry editorial weight; readers form opinions about reviewers, not just about reviews.

## What Should Translate To MixHouse

- Critical-writing-as-service register for any MixHouse music-criticism surface (e.g., cycle reviews, season retrospectives).
- Reviewer-byline visibility for editorial credibility across any opinionated content.
- Breadcrumb-and-tab depth routing where MixHouse surfaces have multiple parallel content streams.

## What Should NOT Translate To MixHouse

- The aggregation-across-many-records architecture (MixHouse is a single franchise; criticism is bounded to MixHouse content and to scene-adjacent content with editorial purpose).
- The cross-promotion-across-tabs duplication pattern.
- The standardised-review-card grid pattern at scale (MixHouse criticism uses long-form editorial treatment for the cycles that warrant it).
