# Apple AirPods Max Marketing Page

## Observed Page Content

Eleven distinct sections cascade vertically: hero with carousel of five colorways stacked in perspective; feature highlights; "Take a closer look" interactive color selector with six detailed breakdowns; "Superior sound down to a science" technical audio narrative; tabbed audio specification section (Microphones, Driver, Distortionless Playback); active noise cancellation feature; communication-features section; battery and convenience features; shopping value propositions; product comparison carousel; full footer.

Headline treatment uses sentence case ("Listening. Remastered."). Display headings carry the dominant hierarchy weight; body copy remains moderate. Five hardware colorways (Midnight, Starlight, Blue, Purple, Orange) drive the visual register; accent colours are otherwise minimal.

Photography includes lifestyle (on-head wear, environmental contexts) and close-up product details (Digital Crown, mesh canopy, ear cushions, aluminum construction). Imagery uses neutral backgrounds emphasising product form. Grid system carries generous vertical spacing; product imagery occupies 40-60% of viewport width per section.

Sample editorial lines: "Listening. Remastered." / "Talk about communication skills." / "Improved high‑fidelity sound. Exhilarating audio with deep bass, expansive mids, and crisp highs."

## Mechanism Analysis

Compared to Vision Pro, this page leans harder on product-photography rhythm and tabbed-interaction surfaces. The mechanism:

1. **Colorway carousel as identity statement.** The five-colour hero is a brand-world statement before it is a feature statement. The grid establishes the personality range before any technical claim.
2. **Tabbed specification surfacing.** Tabbed interaction sections compress technical depth without flattening it. Users who want depth get it; users who want story keep scrolling.
3. **Punchy short editorial sentences.** Two-word headlines ("Listening. Remastered.") with high editorial density. Restraint paired with confidence.

## What Should Translate To MixHouse

- Two-word headline discipline for hero moments on public surfaces.
- Tabbed depth-on-demand for technical content (production details, music-rights structure) that some audiences want and others do not.
- Lifestyle-and-detail photography pairing pattern: environment-of-use plus craft-detail close-up, alternating through the page.
- Carousel as identity-range statement when MixHouse season identity or cycle identity warrants a range-presentation.

## What Should NOT Translate To MixHouse

- The "Buy" CTA pattern on public surfaces (MixHouse does not transact; the show is the product).
- The shopping value propositions section pattern (financing, delivery, specialist guidance); not relevant to MixHouse surfaces.
- The product-comparison carousel pattern.
- Sentence-case-only headlines without editorial variation (MixHouse copy register supports stronger sentence shape than pure product-marketing pacing).
