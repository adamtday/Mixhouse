# Apple Vision Pro Marketing Page

## Observed Page Content

Modular, full-width section pattern: hero image and headline, then sequential thematic blocks (Entertainment, Productivity, Photos and Videos, Connection, Apps, visionOS, Technology, Values), each containing expandable drawer components with imagery and descriptive text. Fixed top navigation with local submenu (Overview, Tech Specs, visionOS, Book Demo).

Display-weight sans-serif anchors hierarchy. Headlines carry substantial leading and weight contrast. Body copy is restrained and functional. Section heads function as transition points between thematic groupings.

Palette is predominantly monochromatic with white and light-gray backgrounds. Product imagery dominates with rich blacks and aluminum finish. Interface elements use Apple's signature blue in CTAs only.

Generous vertical rhythm between sections. Two-column drawer layouts alternate text and imagery. Hero occupies full viewport depth. Whitespace isolates feature blocks. Left-aligned text with centered imagery produces a predictable visual cadence.

Interaction described: drawers expand on tap, scroll reveals sequential sections, "View in your space" triggers AR preview, Guest User interface allows shared access with View Mirroring.

Sample editorial line: "The era of spatial computing is here."

## Mechanism Analysis

The page operates on three reinforcing decisions:

1. **Held-then-reveal scroll pacing.** Each section establishes its frame, holds, then reveals supporting depth via drawer expansion. The pacing is user-driven, not auto-playing.
2. **Display-type discipline as hierarchy.** Hierarchy is constructed entirely through weight and scale of a single type family. There is no decorative typography, no colour-coded hierarchy, no badge or icon language doing hierarchy work.
3. **Restraint as confidence signal.** The product photography carries the visual load. Copy is short, specific, and functional. The page demonstrates premium status by what it omits (no testimonials, no comparison tables, no engagement-bait modals) as much as by what it includes.

## What Should Translate To MixHouse

- Scroll-as-narrative pacing for `08_website_strategy/page_briefs/the_world.md` and the home page.
- Held-then-reveal rhythm for `Drop Night` storytelling and finale sequencing on public surfaces.
- Single-display-family typographic hierarchy with weight-and-scale-only progression.
- Restraint discipline: no testimonials, no comparison tables, no engagement-bait modals on public surfaces.

## What Should NOT Translate To MixHouse

- The product-feature-list architecture (MixHouse leads with character and place, not feature inventory).
- The product-as-hero framing.
- The corporate-footer information density (MixHouse footer carries editorial restraint, not ecosystem-link density).
- The drawer-expansion pattern as default (MixHouse uses chapter-marker scroll, not interactive drawers, for narrative content).
