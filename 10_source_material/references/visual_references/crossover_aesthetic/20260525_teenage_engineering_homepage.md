# Teenage Engineering Homepage

## Observed Page Content

Minimalist grid system with restrained white space. Primary navigation spans horizontally (product categories, instruments, loudspeakers, designs, store). Secondary menu reveals collections via expandable product cards. Modular product blocks arranged in vertical stacks rather than dense grids.

Brand signals sophistication through sparse, sans-serif typesetting. Product names use alphanumeric nomenclature (EP-133, PO-12, OB-4): a technical, almost scientific register that contradicts the playful product ecosystem. Headings appear minimal, all-lowercase or abbreviated, reinforcing restraint.

Voice balances playful irreverence with technical precision:
- Example: "riddim n' ting" pairs colloquial phonetic spelling with minimalist product naming.
- Mission statement: "our mission is to produce quality, functional design products using cutting-edge engineering"
- Interaction labels stay utilitarian ("buy now", "explore all products").

Product photography: thumbnail product imagery dominates (128px icons arranged in clean rows). This flattens product heroics, treating instruments as catalogical objects rather than lifestyle statements. No lifestyle photography; pure product presentation.

Footer contains Japanese text alongside English (global reach signal). Returns policy ("14 day return policy") and shipping thresholds appear matter-of-factly. Address precision (Stockholm coordinates) reinforces brand authenticity.

## Mechanism Analysis

Teenage Engineering's brand world is one of the strongest pattern-references for MixHouse to study:

1. **All-lowercase-minimal-headings as voice.** The typographic restraint is the voice. Capitalisation is intentional and rare.
2. **Alphanumeric product naming.** EP-133, PO-12, OB-4 signal technical competence and refuse lifestyle marketing. The naming convention is itself a brand statement.
3. **Product-as-catalogue-object framing.** No lifestyle photography. The product carries its own meaning; lifestyle context would dilute the brand's confident position.
4. **Playful-technical voice pairing.** "Riddim n' ting" alongside "cutting-edge engineering" demonstrates that scene-fluent voice and technical precision are not opposites; both can exist in the same brand world.

## What Should Translate To MixHouse

- All-lowercase-minimal-headings discipline as a voice consideration for specific MixHouse surfaces (not adopted wholesale, considered as a register option for technical or behind-the-scenes content).
- Playful-and-technical voice pairing: scene-fluent register alongside production-precision register can coexist within a single MixHouse surface.
- Catalogue-object framing for content that is itself the message (cycle archives, mix series, residency calendars).
- Matter-of-fact functional copy for utility surfaces (FAQ, contact, terms) that refuses marketing-page language.

## What Should NOT Translate To MixHouse

- The thumbnail-icon-grid product display pattern (MixHouse content warrants larger, more cinematic presentation than 128px icons).
- The "buy now" / e-commerce CTA pattern (MixHouse does not transact on public surfaces).
- The Stockholm-address-precision signal (cute for TE, not for MixHouse).
- The wholesale all-lowercase-headings adoption (MixHouse needs occasional editorial-display moments that TE's brand refuses; the discipline does not transfer wholesale).
