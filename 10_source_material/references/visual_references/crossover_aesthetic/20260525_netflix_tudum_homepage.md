# Netflix Tudum Homepage

## Observed Page Content

Editorial hub bridging entertainment news with exclusive production content. Functions as Netflix's official behind-the-scenes magazine. Modular grid layout emphasises visual storytelling. Large hero imagery dominates above-the-fold positioning. Content cards cascade vertically. Horizontal primary navigation.

Wordmark "Tudum" references Netflix's distinctive audio signature, signalling playful insider identity. Typography balances sans-serif modernity with approachable warmth; the register signals insider access rather than gatekeeping.

Content clusters reported (per observation): cast interviews, production design breakdowns, behind-the-scenes photography, trending show and film commentary. Organisation appears chronological with thematic tagging.

Photography prioritises candid, on-set moments and talent portraiture over polished studio work, reinforcing the "go behind the streams" intimate-access premise.

Footer: social links, archive filters, cross-promotion to Netflix platforms.

## Mechanism Analysis

Tudum's mechanism is the most relevant single reference for MixHouse companion editorial surfaces:

1. **Branded as adjacent property, not as marketing.** Tudum has its own wordmark and editorial identity. It is not "Netflix marketing"; it is "Tudum, the behind-the-scenes magazine." This separation gives it editorial credibility that a marketing page cannot earn.
2. **On-set photography as the brand world's currency.** Candid production imagery does the brand work. The implicit promise: we are taking you behind the surface.
3. **Per-show or per-cycle entry points.** Content organisation makes show-by-show or franchise-by-franchise entry natural; Tudum becomes a destination for fans of a specific show.

## What Should Translate To MixHouse

- Adjacent-editorial-property pattern: any MixHouse behind-the-scenes surface should have its own editorial identity, not be framed as marketing.
- On-set and behind-the-scenes photography as currency. The promise of intimate access is delivered through imagery first.
- Per-cycle and per-producer entry points so fans of a specific producer can navigate to their material directly.

## What Should NOT Translate To MixHouse

- The Netflix wordmark adjacency framing (MixHouse is the brand; there is no parent platform to adjacent off).
- The cast-interview-as-marketing framing (MixHouse cast emerge from the cycle; interview-style content has different cadence and editorial weight).
- The aggregate-across-many-shows architecture (MixHouse is a single franchise; the editorial surface has format-specific architecture).
