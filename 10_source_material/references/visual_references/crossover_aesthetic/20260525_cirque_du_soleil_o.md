# Cirque du Soleil O Show Page

## Observed Page Content

Hero opens with atmospheric quote emphasising artistic precision: "They respect every little detail. What they do is so very difficult..." This establishes the show's calibre before introducing the page's purpose (unveiling "O's" enchanting world).

Page architecture: descending structure of inspirational quote, then blog introduction, then behind-the-scenes content sections, then FAQ accordion. Navigation consistent with Cirque du Soleil's broader site infrastructure.

Copy register alternates between promotional ("enchanting world", "wonder, and infinite possibilities") and informational (technical FAQs). Editorial voice emphasises artistry and spectacle.

FAQ section dominates with concrete details:
- 90-minute duration, no intermission
- 1.5 million gallons of water in a 25-foot-deep pool
- 75 artists, 130 technicians, 8 Olympians involved
- October 15, 1998 premiere; 20+ million spectators total

Hierarchical footer organises corporate divisions (Careers, Sustainability), related entertainment properties (Blue Man Group), legal / accessibility compliance.

Photography is minimal in this observation; experiential content emphasised through referenced web series exploring production elements.

## Mechanism Analysis

The "O" show page reveals Cirque's per-show treatment:

1. **Third-party quote as opening anchor.** The page leads with an external voice, not with marketing copy. The choice signals credibility by deferring to outside testimony.
2. **Spectacle-by-numbers FAQ.** Specific scale numbers (1.5 million gallons, 75 artists, 130 technicians, 20+ million spectators) replace abstract claims. The page lets quantification do the persuasion.
3. **Per-show editorial framing within a brand-world parent.** "O" is presented as its own world inside the larger Cirque brand. The framing supports a multi-show franchise without each show feeling like an SKU.

## What Should Translate To MixHouse

- Third-party-quote-as-opening discipline for any MixHouse cycle or season page once advisor / judge / press testimony exists. Credibility comes from external voices.
- Spectacle-by-numbers FAQ pattern for any depth-content surface where specific scale numbers (production scale, cycle scale, residency scale) carry persuasion that abstract copy cannot.
- Per-cycle-editorial-framing within a brand-world parent: each MixHouse cycle is presented as its own world inside the larger franchise, not as a season inventory item.

## What Should NOT Translate To MixHouse

- The aspirational copy ("enchanting world", "wonder, and infinite possibilities"); same critique as the Cirque homepage; this register reads as marketing-pace.
- The FAQ-as-dominant-page-section pattern at default (MixHouse depth content uses editorial long-form rather than FAQ-accordion structure for most surfaces).
- The minimal-photography pattern on a show page (MixHouse cycle pages lead with strong cinematic imagery, not with text-and-FAQ).
