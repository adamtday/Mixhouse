# Formula 1 Drivers Page

## Observed Page Content

Comprehensive header navigation: Schedule, Results, News, Drivers, Teams, Fantasy & Gaming. Top bar with F1 Authentics, Store, Tickets, Hospitality, Experiences, F1 TV.

Primary focus: grid-based driver roster displaying all 20 competitors for the 2026 season. Each driver card includes portrait photograph, name, team affiliation, national flag. Clean card format with consistent sizing.

Information hierarchy:
- Current race schedule (Canada Round 5, Monaco Round 6)
- Recent news items with thumbnail images
- Video highlights from the latest Grand Prix
- Complete driver listings organised by team

Short, energetic news headlines:
- "We can do better" attributed to Verstappen pushing Red Bull despite podium
- "Norris admits Canada retirement 'put us out of our misery.'"

Typography: clean sans-serif emphasising driver names and team names. Photography dominates: each driver receives professional headshots against team-colour backgrounds.

Footer: extensive partner logos (LVMH, Pirelli, Aramco, AWS), app download buttons, social media (Facebook, X, Instagram, YouTube).

## Mechanism Analysis

The Drivers page is the strongest single reference for MixHouse cast-presentation:

1. **Twenty-driver-roster-as-grid.** The grid is the page. Audiences scan all 20 drivers at once; no editorial hierarchy ranks them above each other.
2. **Team-colour-background portraiture.** Each driver headshot is shot against their team's colour. The treatment is consistent across the roster and visually unifies portraiture even as drivers change teams.
3. **National-flag metadata.** Country flags appear per driver, surfacing geographic identity. Audiences identify drivers partly by nationality; the metadata respects that.
4. **Roster-as-permanent-page.** Drivers do not appear as content; they appear as fixtures. The page persists across the season; it updates as the roster changes.

## What Should Translate To MixHouse

- Roster-as-grid pattern for MixHouse cast presentation (producers, mentors, judges). All cast appear at once; no editorial ranking on the roster page.
- Consistent-treatment portraiture across the cast roster (single photographer, single direction, single colour or background system per role).
- Geographic or sub-genre metadata per cast member surfacing scene-context fluently.
- Roster-as-permanent-page for cast: the page persists across cycles, updates as cast rotates, never feels like content-of-the-week.

## What Should NOT Translate To MixHouse

- The team-colour-coding pattern (MixHouse producers are not on team rosters; the colour-coding does not transfer).
- The dense partner-logo footer pattern (same critique as the F1 home page).
- The Fantasy / Gaming top-navigation entry.
- The news-headline-with-driver-quote pattern as a primary content layer for the roster page (the roster page is permanent reference; news lives elsewhere).
