# Casting Pipeline

## Purpose

Define the casting funnel that supplies MixHouse with scene-credible, character-rich producers and DJs — from initial outreach through final ensemble selection — and the operational metrics that govern its quality.

## Key Takeaways

- Casting is the format's most strategic operational asset. Funnel quality determines season quality.
- Three intake channels: scene-partner referrals, open call, scout team. Each balances quality vs scale.
- Casting is for character + craft + scene credibility, in that order.
- Funnel target: 500+ qualified applicants per pilot / season, narrowed to 12–16 for ensemble cast.

## Core Content

### Three Intake Channels

| Channel | Role | Target volume |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Scene partner referrals** | Highest-quality, lowest-volume. Promoters, labels, agencies, advisors refer named producers. | 50–100 referrals. |
| **Open call** | Highest-volume, mixed-quality. Public application via website with structured submission. | 1,000–5,000 applications. |
| **Scout team** | Targeted outreach to specific producers we want in. | 50–150 named targets. |

The funnel deliberately stacks scene partners on top — they unlock applicants who would not apply cold.

### Stage Gates

```
Stage 0 — Awareness        (campaign, scene partners reach out)
Stage 1 — Application      (submission via site / referral)
Stage 2 — Qualification    (does the applicant clear the three filters?)
Stage 3 — Video round      (short video + recent track submission)
Stage 4 — Studio session   (in-person production task; filmed)
Stage 5 — Chemistry test   (with shortlisted ensemble candidates)
Stage 6 — Cast offer       (contract + rights deal + onboarding)
```

### The Three Filters

Every applicant must clear:

1. **Character** — magnetic on camera; voiced opinion; ability to carry a confessional.
2. **Craft** — releasable production quality; can finish a track in a week.
3. **Scene credibility** — released real music, played real gigs, or vouched for by named scene actors.

Any one of the three failing disqualifies. No "potential" exceptions.

### Quality Metrics

| Metric | Target |
| --- | --- |
| Qualified applicants per casting cycle | ≥ 500 |
| Referral share of cast | ≥ 50% of final ensemble |
| Geographic diversity (regions represented in cast) | ≥ 4 |
| Sub-genre diversity in cast | ≥ 5 |
| Drop-out rate post-offer | ≤ 10% |

### Casting Partners

| Partner type | Role |
| --- | --- |
| Labels (Defected, Drumcode, Anjuna, Ninja Tune, etc.) | Referral source. |
| Promoters (Hï, fabric, Womb, Output legacy networks) | Referral source. |
| Agencies (WME, CAA, UTA music) | Referral source for emerging producers under deal. |
| Music tech (Splice, Native Instruments) | Distribution of open call to producer base. |
| Scene media (Resident Advisor, Mixmag, DJ Mag) | Reach amplification. |

### Casting Materials

- Public site casting page (call-to-action; clear rights summary).
- Submission portal (track + 60s video + bio + scene references).
- Scene-partner referral one-pager (concise pitch for promoters/labels).
- Talent rights summary (plain-English version of `05_business_model/music_rights_strategy.md`).

### Anti-Patterns

- "Reality regulars" with multi-show histories. Disqualifying.
- Audition-tape candidates with no gigging / releasing history. Disqualifying.
- Cast contestants without contract-grade rights disclosure. Disqualifying.
- Visible bias toward already-famous applicants over scene-credible emerging ones.

### Onboarding (Pre-Production)

Once cast:

- Rights contracts signed.
- Mental health, on-camera media training, and platform safety briefings.
- Gear orientation and residency-studio walkthrough.
- Confidentiality and conduct briefings.

### Funnel Reuse Across Seasons

The casting database is a compounding asset (see `05_business_model/franchise_flywheel.md`). Past applicants who didn't make it are not deleted; they re-enter future cycles if eligible.

## Objections Answered

| Objection | Response |
| --- | --- |
| "Open call risks unqualified noise." | Stage gates filter noise. Scene-partner referrals do most of the quality lifting. |
| "Scene-credible producers won't apply." | They will if the format respects them. The rights deal and advisor list make the case before the camera rolls. |
