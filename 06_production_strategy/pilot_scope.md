# Pilot Scope

## Purpose

Define the scope, deliverables, and success criteria of the MixHouse pilot, the minimum production unit that proves the format works on camera, in market, and through the release pipeline.

## Key Takeaways

- The pilot is a 2-week production block delivering a watchable episode (or 2-episode block), 2 `Drop Nights`, and 6–10 commercially released tracks.
- Pilot deliverables must include sponsor-grade integration evidence and short-form distribution outputs.
- Success criteria are measurable: completion rate, save rate, sponsor demand, advisor sign-off.
- The pilot does not require a flagship venue or a tier-1 mentor, it requires proof of format coherence.

## Core Content

### Pilot Deliverables

| Deliverable | Spec |
| --- | --- |
| **Cinematic episode (or 2-ep block)** | ~50-min cut following the five-act anatomy in `02_show_bible/episode_anatomy.md`. |
| **`Drop Nights`** | 2 live events at scene-credible mid-cap venues; full multi-cam capture. |
| **Released tracks** | 6–10 finished, mastered, distributed across DSPs under the pilot label structure. |
| **Short-form reel** | A 60–90s sizzle plus 12+ vertical clips for short-form platforms. |
| **Sponsor proof-of-concept** | At least one category integration filmed end-to-end (e.g. studio gear partner). |
| **Press pack** | EPK, photo, bios, advisor list, music links. |

### Pilot Cast Size

- 6–8 contestants for the pilot (smaller than a full season's 12–16).
- All cleared rights, scene-credible, willing to be filmed at production-grade cinematography.

### Production Block

- **Duration:** 12–14 days on-location.
- **Location:** smaller residency than a flagship, see `06_production_strategy/location_strategy.md` for options.
- **Cinematic team:** scripted-grade DP, 3-camera baseline, drone, intimate handheld.
- **Music team:** 2 mentor sessions; supervising A&R; 1–2 supervising engineers.
- **Live team:** rigged-in-advance `Drop Night` venue with multi-cam and audience-reaction tooling.

### Success Criteria (Definition of Done)

| Criterion | Measure |
| --- | --- |
| Episode completion | Audience completion rate ≥ 65% on the streamer-ready cut. |
| Save / pre-save rate | ≥ X% of viewers add at least one track to a DSP library. (X to be defined.) |
| Sponsor demand | ≥ 3 LOIs from non-overlapping categories. |
| Advisor sign-off | ≥ 5 scene advisors publicly endorse the pilot. |
| Music performance | Average per-track first-72-hour streams above pilot-cohort threshold. |
| Cultural authenticity check | Independent scene press review ≥ neutral on tone. |

These thresholds are calibrated in the pilot and become the S1 benchmarks.

### What the Pilot Does Not Need

- A tier-1 festival headline (`Final Set` is an S1 commitment, not a pilot demand).
- A global anchor sponsor.
- A full territory rollout plan.
- A live broadcast.

### Pilot vs Broadcast Pilot

This pilot is a *proof-of-format pilot*, used for investor, broadcaster, sponsor, and advisor demonstration. A broadcaster may subsequently request a separate "broadcast pilot" once committed; the proof-of-format pilot informs that block's scope.

### Risks During Pilot

See `06_production_strategy/production_risks.md`. The pilot-specific risks:

| Risk | Mitigation |
| --- | --- |
| Insufficient music quality on Day 1 | Mentor heavy involvement in first week; supervisor pre-vets contestant catalogues. |
| Cast member withdrawal | Maintain a standby pair pre-cleared. |
| Venue / weather issues | Indoor backup `Drop Night` venue locked. |
| Sponsor integration falls through | Two parallel integration tracks, only one needs to deliver. |

### Cost Posture

- Pilot all-in cost benchmarked against prestige unscripted pilot economics.
- Itemised use of funds in `06_production_strategy/use_of_funds.md`.
- Music release costs ring-fenced from production costs.

## Objections Answered

| Objection | Response |
| --- | --- |
| "Pilots burn capital without revenue." | Pilots de-risk the much larger S1 commitment and unlock sponsor LOIs that can't be earned without filmed proof. |
| "Why not skip to S1?" | Because S1 financing closes faster with a pilot in market. The pilot is an investment vehicle, not a cost centre. |
