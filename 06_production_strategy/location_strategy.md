# Location Strategy

## Purpose

Evaluate candidate residency cities and venues for the pilot and Season 1, with the trade-offs between scene credibility, production cost, climate, and brand identity made explicit.

## Key Takeaways

- The residency *is* a character. Location choice shapes brand more than any single design decision.
- Six tier-1 city candidates; final choice depends on scene credibility, production tax incentives, summer-season alignment, and security of festival partner for `Final Set`.
- The location strategy supports the franchise's territory adaptation model — each adaptation rotates location while keeping the format intact.

## Core Content

### Evaluation Axes

| Axis | What it tests |
| --- | --- |
| Scene credibility | Does the city read as a real electronic-music capital? |
| Talent depth | Can we cast from local + global producers willing to relocate? |
| Production infrastructure | Crew, gear, venues, tax incentives. |
| Climate / window | Does the shoot window align with the residency season? |
| Cost | All-in cost relative to comparable shoot. |
| Festival partner proximity | Plausible `Final Set` festival anchor in driving distance. |
| Visual identity | Does the location have a signature visual brand? |
| Safety / logistics | Visa, security, talent comfort. |

### Tier-1 Candidates

| City | Scene | Visual identity | Cost | Festival anchor | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Ibiza** | Tier-1 global. | Sun, white, sea, golden hour. | High. | DC10 / Ushuaïa / Hï residencies; Tomorrowland (BE) day-trip. | Peak season demand strains availability. |
| **Berlin** | Tier-1 techno. | Industrial, monochrome, raw. | Medium-low. | Berghain residency; CTM, Berlin Atonal. | Year-round shoot window. |
| **Mexico City / Tulum** | Rapidly rising. | Tropical, brutalist, mystical. | Medium. | EDC México; Bahidorá; SXM Festival (Caribbean). | Strong producer-as-celebrity scene. |
| **Amsterdam** | Tier-1 dance. | Canals, modern brutalist, neon. | Medium-high. | ADE; DGTL; Awakenings. | Production-friendly. |
| **Lisbon** | Rising. | Pastel, ocean, hilltop. | Medium-low. | NOS Alive / Boom (electronic adjacent). | Lower cost, growing scene. |
| **Athens / Mykonos** | Rising. | Ancient + ultra-modern. | Medium. | Plissken; Cavo Paradiso. | Visual ambition, infrastructure caveat. |

### Tier-2 / Future Candidates

- Tokyo (territory adaptation).
- Mumbai / Goa (territory adaptation).
- Cape Town (territory adaptation).
- Lagos (territory adaptation; afro house is one of the fastest-growing sub-genres).
- São Paulo (territory adaptation).

### Pilot Recommendation Posture

The pilot likely lives in a **Berlin or Lisbon** style location for cost and year-round shootability. The flagship S1 likely lives in **Ibiza or Mexico City** for brand spectacle and festival anchor proximity. To be confirmed pending broadcaster preference.

### Production Infrastructure Decision Factors

- Tax incentive structure (EU vs UK vs LatAm).
- Crew unionisation rules.
- Production insurance feasibility.
- Visa logistics for international cast.

### Cinematic Identity Per Location

Each candidate location has a defined visual identity that flows into `07_brand_world/visual_positioning.md`. Choice of location commits the brand to that identity for the season.

### Territory Adaptation Mechanic

When MixHouse franchises to a new territory, the local production partner proposes a residency city following the same evaluation axes. Format Co. approves; the bible stays consistent.
