# Business Model Page Brief

## Purpose

Define the page that explains, at public-defensible depth, why MixHouse is a multi-line franchise rather than a single-season format — and routes serious inquiries into the Materials gate.

## Key Takeaways

- L3 (Substance).
- Audiences: investor, broadcaster, sponsor, advisor, scene press.
- Carries A/B-cited evidence only. Confidential figures live in Materials (L4).
- The franchise flywheel diagram is the page's centrepiece.

## Core Content

### Disclosure Layer

L3 (Substance).

### Page Outline

1. **Hero — The thesis in one paragraph.**
   - Editorial. From `04_competitive_landscape/mixhouse_white_space.md` (the White Space Statement).
2. **Why now.**
   - Three numbered conditions with one A/B-cited stat each. Source: `03_market_research/why_now.md`.
3. **Where MixHouse sits.**
   - Competitive matrix table. Source: `04_competitive_landscape/competitive_matrix.md`.
4. **How MixHouse compounds.**
   - Franchise flywheel diagram. Source: `05_business_model/franchise_flywheel.md`.
5. **Five revenue lines.**
   - Stack visualisation + one-paragraph each. Source: `05_business_model/revenue_model.md`.
6. **Live as a primary line.**
   - Source: `05_business_model/live_event_extensions.md`.
7. **The platform layer.**
   - Source: `05_business_model/platform_extensions.md`.
8. **Sponsorship inventory.**
   - Category-exclusive integration principles (no pricing). Source: `05_business_model/sponsorship_inventory.md`.
9. **The case in one paragraph.**
   - Closer paragraph; not a sales pitch.
10. **Materials gate.**
    - For investor / broadcaster / sponsor depth.
