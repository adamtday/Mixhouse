# Materials Page Brief

## Purpose

Define the gated surface through which investors, broadcasters, and sponsors request access to confidential MixHouse materials.

## Key Takeaways

- L4 (Gated).
- Single, well-designed form. Audience-segmented routing under the hood.
- Materials are delivered after manual approval, not auto-issued.
- The page itself remains on-brand — gating does not break the editorial atmosphere.

## Core Content

### Disclosure Layer

L4 (Gated).

### Page Outline

1. **Editorial intro.**
   - One short paragraph: "Materials are shared after a short request." No friction-shaming.
2. **The form.**
   - Required: name, organisation, email, audience type (investor / broadcaster / sponsor / press / other), one-line context.
   - Optional: introducer name, scheduled call link.
3. **What you receive (per audience).**
   - Investor: deck + financial summary + use of funds + cap table summary (NDA on access).
   - Broadcaster: format pack + sizzle reel + cast + advisor list (NDA on access).
   - Sponsor: inventory + integration philosophy + category exclusivity terms (NDA on access).
   - Press: EPK with embargoed elements clearly marked.
4. **Confidentiality terms.**
   - Plain-English summary of NDA terms. Link to full document.
5. **Approval process.**
   - "We respond within [X] business days." Realistic, not aspirational.

### Audience Priority

| Order | Audience | What they get |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Investor / broadcaster / sponsor | Direct path to materials. |
| 2 | Press | EPK access. |
| 3 | Other | Routed to appropriate contact. |

### Tone

- Quiet, professional, editorial.
- No marketing.
- Confidentiality framed as standard practice, not a hurdle.

### Components Used

- EditorialSpread (intro)
- MaterialsGate (form + CRM integration)
- Microcopy components

### Form Design

- Single-page form with clear segmentation by audience type.
- Audience-specific follow-up questions revealed dynamically.
- Email confirmation auto-sent on submission.
- Approval and material delivery handled by Format Co. team manually.

### NDA Handling

Two options:
1. Click-through NDA at submission (faster but less defensible).
2. Counter-signed NDA via document partner (DocuSign / Dropbox Sign) before materials are delivered.

Recommended: option 2 for investor and broadcaster; option 1 for press and sponsor first-look.

### CRM Integration

- All submissions tagged by audience and source.
- Materials delivered tracked per audience.
- Inactive requests followed up at defined intervals.

### Performance and Media Targets

- LCP < 2.5s.
- Form completion targets to be measured.
- No tracking beyond consented analytics + form completion event.

### Security

- All gated materials stored in access-controlled storage.
- Watermarked PDFs for investor / broadcaster decks.
- Materials versioned; old versions automatically expire.

## Objections Answered

| Objection | Response |
| --- | --- |
| "Manual approval slows things down." | It also makes the deal-flow legible and protects materials. Faster lanes exist for warm intros via direct outreach. |
| "Why not just publish?" | Because public numbers anchor incorrectly and reduce optionality. |

## Website Implications

- This page exists in launch mode. Its design language matches the rest of the site.
- The form is the page's main interactive surface and must be flawlessly usable.
