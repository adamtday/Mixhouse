# Live Event Extensions

## Purpose

Define the year-round live-event surface that extends MixHouse beyond the season's linear/streamer cut and turns the IP into a touring, ticketed business.

## Key Takeaways

- Live events are not promotional add-ons — they are a primary revenue line and the most reliable on-ramp from viewer to fan.
- Four live extensions: `Final Set` festival headline, finalists' post-season tour, alumni live activations, branded MixHouse live experiences.
- Live ticketing economics are well-understood; the franchise margins benefit from owning the IP that fills the venue.
- Each live extension feeds the next season's casting and audience funnel.

## Core Content

### The Four Live Extensions

#### 1. Final Set — Festival Headline

- The season finale's headline performance.
- Hosted at a real partner festival in front of a real festival audience.
- Filmed as the season's culminating set piece and as a future cinematic livestream.
- Co-branded with festival partner and franchise anchor sponsor.

Operational owner: `03_market_research/festival_live_experience_market.md`, `06_production_strategy/location_strategy.md`.

#### 2. Finalists' Tour

- Multi-city touring package for finalists immediately post-season.
- Venue tier: scene-credible mid-cap (1,500–5,000 cap) rooms.
- 6–12 city run depending on territory.
- Promoted by MixHouse Live with regional promoter partners.

#### 3. Alumni Live Activations

- Annual MixHouse Live events featuring multi-season alumni.
- Anchor cities (London, Berlin, NYC, LA, CDMX, São Paulo, Tokyo).
- Programmed as cultural calendar fixtures (e.g. ADE week, Miami WMC).

#### 4. Branded MixHouse Live Experiences

- Year-round branded live activations licensed to festivals, clubs, or brand partners.
- Lower-margin per-event but high-frequency.
- Maintain franchise audience attention between seasons.

### Why Live Is Strategic, Not Promotional

- **Highest fan-conversion channel.** First gig converts streamer-viewer into long-term fan.
- **Talent durability.** Touring careers outlast TV cycles; supports the franchise's "real artist" claim.
- **Sponsorship value.** Live activations price higher than equivalent show integrations.
- **Territory leverage.** Live is the proof-point for format-sale conversations.

### Ticketing and Owned Audience

- Pre-sales and priority ticketing routed through the MixHouse app / CRM (see `02_show_bible/fan_participation.md`).
- Email + push notification triggers tied to viewing behaviour.
- Sponsor exclusivity bundled into ticket experiences (e.g. partner-presented bar).

### Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
| --- | --- |
| Weather / outdoor finale | Insurance + contingency venue. |
| Ticket-demand miss | Conservative venue tier for S1; scale up with proven demand. |
| Talent withdrawal | Multiple finalists across tour; alumni backups. |
| Promoter dependency | Multi-promoter relationships across territories. |

### Integration with the Format

- `Final Set` is the season's narrative resolution.
- Tour is the narrative epilogue and the start of the next casting funnel.
- Alumni activations are the franchise's connective tissue across seasons.

## Objections Answered

| Objection | Response |
| --- | --- |
| "Live is operationally heavy." | MixHouse Live is a separate operational stack with promoter partners. The format company doesn't operate every room. |
| "Reality-TV alumni tours often flop." | Singers do. Electronic producers/DJs have built-in performance careers — the tour is upgraded scale, not invented economics. |
