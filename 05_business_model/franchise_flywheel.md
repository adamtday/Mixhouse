# Franchise Flywheel

## Purpose

Describe — in mechanism, not metaphor — how each MixHouse season makes the next season cheaper to make, more valuable to commission, and more profitable to operate. This is the central investment thesis.

## Key Takeaways

- Each season produces seven re-usable assets: catalogue, alumni, format playbook, brand IP, casting funnel, audience CRM, advisor network.
- These assets are durable across seasons and territories.
- The flywheel converts one-off production economics into compounding format-company economics.
- The franchise reaches steady-state value when format sales begin closing in year 2–3.

## Core Content

### The Seven Compounding Assets

| Asset | What it is | How it compounds |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Catalogue** | Owned (in part) music releases from each season. | Catalogue earns royalties across all territories indefinitely; new seasons add to the stack. |
| **Alumni** | Past contestants with growing followings. | Alumni anchor live tours, return as mentors, headline finalist showcases. |
| **Format Playbook** | The operationalised production and music release pipeline. | Each season de-risks the next; territory adaptations inherit the playbook. |
| **Brand IP** | The MixHouse name, visual identity, and audience trust. | Brand recognition reduces marketing cost per acquired viewer/sponsor. |
| **Casting Funnel** | Producer/DJ database, scoring history, scene relationships. | Casting cost per season falls; quality rises. |
| **Audience CRM** | Email/app/follower database tied to releases and tour. | Direct distribution channel for new music, tickets, merch. |
| **Advisor Network** | Scene-credible advisors, judges, mentors. | Reputational moat that compounds with every season they appear. |

### The Mechanism

Each season:
1. **Spends** on production, music releases, live events.
2. **Captures** seven asset increments (above).
3. **Releases** in market — music, episodes, live events.
4. **Feeds** the next season — cheaper casting, deeper catalogue, broader audience, stronger advisor network, format-sale readiness.

This is a literal flywheel: input → asset stock → leverage on next input.

### Why It Compounds (Not Just Adds)

- **Catalogue:** royalties stack non-additively as catalogue increases discoverability.
- **Alumni:** their growing audiences become marketing channels for the show.
- **Brand IP:** reach scales non-linearly with brand recognition.
- **Casting Funnel:** known reputation pulls top-tier applicants who would not have applied to season 1.
- **Format Sales:** territory adaptations create per-territory versions of all seven assets.

### Steady State

When the franchise hits year 3–4 with:
- 3+ active territories.
- Catalogue of 200+ tracks.
- A live touring stack with annual cadence.
- Sponsorship contracts across 3+ categories at category-exclusive rates.

...the format-company looks closer to a music publisher or live promoter in valuation than a one-off TV production company.

### What Breaks the Flywheel

| Breaker | How to mitigate |
| --- | --- |
| Season 1 underperforms commercially | Multi-cycle production financing with break clauses; sponsorship LOIs not fully reliant on S1 ratings. |
| Music IP rights deals fall through | Clean rights structure pre-locked at casting; multiple label partners. |
| Cultural authenticity erodes | Advisor + judge governance, anti-pattern enforcement (see `07_brand_world/`). |
| Talent backlash | Transparent rights deal, no villain edits, real artist value (see `02_show_bible/judging_framework.md`). |

## Objections Answered

| Objection | Response |
| --- | --- |
| "Flywheels are easy to draw, hard to spin." | We name the inputs (seven assets), the mechanism, and the steady-state — and we tie each to a specific operational file. |
| "Music IP value depends on hits." | Catalogue depth de-risks. Even modest per-track performance across 60+ tracks per season per territory accumulates meaningfully. |
