# Investor Case

## Purpose

State the investment thesis at the level of a memo: opportunity, business, founders, financing ask, use of funds, milestones, and exit. Everything an investor needs to decide whether to take a follow-on meeting after a 20-minute first call.

## Key Takeaways

- The opportunity: a multi-decade music-led franchise built at the intersection of three existing categories with no incumbent occupying the gap.
- The business: a format company with diversified revenue (production fee + music IP + live + sponsorship + format sales).
- The ask: financing for pilot + S1 + platform with milestone-gated tranches.
- The exit: format-company acquisition by a streamer, major label, or music-tech buyer at year 4–6; alternatively, durable cash-flow business.
- All figures in this file are placeholders pending the financial model.

## Core Content

### Opportunity

See `01_master_context/mixhouse_master_thesis.md`. In one paragraph:

> Electronic music is now globally mainstream by audience size, festival economy, and producer-as-celebrity profile, but no music format has been built to serve it. The slot at the intersection of prestige cinematic unscripted, music creation, and live-event franchise has remained empty because no operator has combined scene credibility with format engineering. MixHouse is that operator.

### Exit Scenarios

| Scenario | Buyer profile | Why they'd buy |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Streamer M&A** | Netflix, Amazon, Apple, Max, Disney. | Recurring prestige unscripted IP with international scaling. |
| **Music major M&A** | UMG, Sony, Warner. | Catalogue value + artist-development pipeline + sync portfolio. |
| **Live conglomerate M&A** | Live Nation, AEG. | Touring franchise + audience CRM. |
| **Music-tech crossover** | Spotify, TikTok parent, AI music co. | Direct music-discovery franchise tied to listening data. |
| **Cash-flow business** | Continue operating independently with dividends. | Cleanest path if no premium offer materialises. |

### Why This Company, Not a Pure TV Bet

- Music IP catalogue value scales with seasons.
- Live touring is a real, ticketed, year-round business.
- Format sales open multi-territory upside.
- Direct platform reduces audience-acquisition risk.

The combined valuation logic is closer to a music + live company than a TV production house.

## Objections Answered

| Objection | Response |
| --- | --- |
| "TV formats aren't venture-scale." | Single-line format companies aren't. Multi-line format + music + live + platform companies are, see comparable music-tech valuations. |
| "You need a hit to make this work." | We need at least one durable hit per season. The format engine increases the surface area for a hit; the catalogue de-risks the dependency on a single track. |
| "Music releases aren't an investor's expertise." | We don't ask investors to A&R. We ask them to back the franchise; A&R is run by named partners. |
