# Sponsorship Inventory

## Purpose

Enumerate the sponsorable surfaces inside the MixHouse franchise, across show, live events, and platform, define category exclusivity rules, and set the principles that protect cultural authenticity.

## Key Takeaways

- Sponsorship is sold as **category-exclusive integration**, not banner inventory.
- Six core categories: audio tech, beverage, fashion, gaming, telco, automotive (luxury / EV).
- Inventory is split across show integration, live events, platform, and franchise (multi-season).
- Cultural authenticity gates every deal, see `07_brand_world/cultural_authenticity.md`.
- Pricing benchmarks pull from festival sponsorship, not TV ad-spot rates.

### Integration Principles

1. **Real product, real use.** If contestants use the product, the brand can integrate. No fake adoption.
2. **Category exclusivity.** One brand per category, locked annually.
3. **No banner reads.** Integration is contextual; voiceover ads are off-limits.
4. **Scene veto.** Resident judges have an effective veto on category fit (e.g. low-credibility energy drinks would not pass).
5. **Talent consent.** Contestants are briefed on integrations pre-cast and consent in writing.

### Category Treatment

| Category | Treatment |
| --- | --- |
| **Audio tech** | Deepest integration potential, studio gear, headphones, monitors. Likely category 1. |
| **Beverage** | High fit, must be scene-credible. Energy / RTD / spirits all in scope. |
| **Fashion** | Wardrobe partner with co-designed capsule per season. |
| **Gaming** | Crossover potential via in-game music drops and creator collab seasons. |
| **Telco** | Platform / live-stream partner with carrier-level distribution. |
| **Automotive (EV / luxury)** | Travel between residency and venues; transport package. |

### Pricing Posture

Pricing benchmarks come from:
- Festival presenting-sponsor rates (e.g. Coachella, Tomorrowland, Lollapalooza presenting deals).
- Sports-docu presenting-sponsor rates.
- Streamer integration rates (Netflix branded content, Amazon original integrations).

Specific numbers to be modelled with brand-partnership advisors and listed in `05_business_model/investor_case.md`.

### Multi-Year Anchor Sponsor

A franchise anchor sponsor (paid annually, multi-territory) is the high-margin top of the inventory. Profile:
- Global brand with credible music-scene history.
- Multi-territory media spend.
- Appetite for multi-year commitments to an unproven flagship.

### What's Not Sponsorable

- Judging or elimination decisions.
- Editorial choice of music releases.
- Contestant casting.
- Anything that conflicts with `07_brand_world/cultural_authenticity.md`.

### Pre-Pilot Sponsor LOIs

- Goal: 3 category LOIs (audio + beverage + one other) before broadcaster commitment.
- LOIs include category exclusivity rights subject to broadcaster sign-off.
- Used as a leading indicator of demo desirability in broadcaster conversations.

## Objections Answered

| Objection | Response |
| --- | --- |
| "Sponsorships in music shows feel cheap." | Cheap because they are bolted on. Ours are integrated at format-design level, scene-vetted, and category-exclusive. |
| "Sponsors will demand creative control." | Our integration principles are non-negotiable. The category-exclusivity premium covers the loss of editorial concessions. |
