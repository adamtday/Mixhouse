# Platform Extensions

## Purpose

Define the platform layer that sits underneath the franchise — app, site, CRM, short-form distribution — and explain how it converts viewers into a directly addressable audience that monetises through music, live, and merchandise.

## Key Takeaways

- The platform is the connective tissue between viewers, listeners, and ticket-buyers. Without it, the franchise leaks audience to third parties on every cycle.
- Core surfaces: public website, MixHouse app, CRM/email, short-form distribution.
- The platform is built for *direct-to-fan* economics: pre-saves, ticket priority, alumni updates, merchandise.
- Platform investment is justified because it persists between seasons.

### The Conversion Funnel the Platform Operates

1. **View** (linear/streamer) → discover MixHouse and its contestants.
2. **Pre-save / save** (DSP) → first explicit intent.
3. **Follow** (app/email) → direct addressability.
4. **Attend** (ticketed live event) → real-money conversion.
5. **Advocate** (share, gift, repeat) → long-tail brand value.

Every platform surface should advance fans along this funnel.

### Why Not Rely on Third-Party Platforms?

- Streamers retain the viewer relationship; we don't get the data.
- DSPs treat us as another release client; algorithm changes hurt economics.
- Short-form platforms own discovery; we own context.

A direct audience layer means we can:
- Monetise multi-season alumni careers.
- Push tickets at scale.
- Sell category-exclusive sponsorships with proven CRM reach.
- Adapt the franchise faster (international rollout) by leveraging existing CRM-confirmed demand.

### Build vs Buy

| Component | Build / Buy |

| App | Phase 2; partner-built or wrapped framework. |
| CRM | Buy (Klaviyo / Customer.io / similar). |
| Streaming integrations | Buy (Linkfire / Feature.fm / similar). |
| Short-form workflow | Build pipeline; buy distribution tooling. |
| Ticketing | Buy via promoter partners (DICE, AXS, Festicket). |

### Phasing

- **Pre-pilot** — public site + CRM + short-form pipeline.
- **Pilot** — pre-save flows + ticketing partner integration.
- **Post-S1** — app launch with watch + follow + save.
- **S2+** — virtual `Drop Night`, alumni tour priority, merchandise store.

### Data Strategy

- First-party email and consented analytics from day 1.
- Music release performance attribution via DSP partners.
- Short-form virality via platform-native analytics.
- Compliance with GDPR / UK GDPR / CPRA from launch.

### What the Platform Avoids

- Becoming a streaming service. We do not host video competing with our distribution partners.
- Owning ticketing primary infrastructure. We partner.
- Owning music distribution infrastructure. We partner.

### Revenue From the Platform

Direct revenue lines:
- Ticketing margin (partner deal).
- Merchandise direct-to-consumer.
- Sponsor placement inside the app.
- Possible premium tier (alumni / fan club) longer-term.

Indirect revenue impact (the bigger number):
- Higher sponsorship pricing (proof of direct audience).
- Higher format-sale pricing per territory (data to underwrite).
- Lower casting cost per qualified applicant.

## Objections Answered

| Objection | Response |
| --- | --- |
| "Why build a platform at all?" | Because the alternative is leaking the entire franchise audience to streamers, DSPs, and platforms — and re-acquiring them every season at full marketing cost. |
| "Apps are expensive to build." | Phasing keeps the investment proportional: web + CRM first, then app once S1 demand justifies. |
