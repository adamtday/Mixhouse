# MixHouse Master Thesis

## Purpose

State the single, load-bearing argument behind MixHouse as a franchise: why this show, why this team, why now, and why this is a multi-decade IP rather than a single-season reality bet. Every other document in this KB should be reducible back to a claim in this file.

## Key Takeaways

- Electronic music is the largest underserved music genre in scripted/unscripted television. Comparable formats exist for hip-hop (`Rhythm + Flow`), pop (`The Voice`), country (`Real Country`) and classical (`The Piano`) — none for electronic music.
- The cultural and commercial conditions of electronic music in 2026 — global festival economy, post-COVID resurgence of clubbing, the producer-as-celebrity model — make this the moment, not 2010 or 2030.
- The format must combine reality-TV character arcs (Love Island stickiness) with documentary cinematography (Drive to Survive prestige) and live-event spectacle (Cercle, Boiler Room) — none of these alone is sufficient.
- The thesis is franchise, not show: MixHouse exists across linear, streamer, short-form, live events, music releases, and brand partnerships from day one.
- Success is measured on cultural penetration of the *music released through the show*, not just ratings — every season produces career-launching artists.

## Core Content

### The Underserved Genre Claim

Electronic music is, by most measures, the second- or third-largest music genre globally by listening, festival attendance, and creator participation. It anchors the world's largest music events (Tomorrowland, Ultra, EDC, Coachella's electronic stages). And yet:

- No mass-market reality format has cracked it.
- DJ documentaries exist (e.g. retrospective on Daft Punk, Avicii) but no recurring tentpole IP.
- The streaming era has produced electronic stars who are functionally invisible to broadcast audiences (Fred again.., Peggy Gou, Bicep).

This is the white space MixHouse occupies. See `04_competitive_landscape/mixhouse_white_space.md` for the full map.

### Why Now

Four converging conditions:

1. **Festival economy at peak.** Live electronic music is a multi-billion-dollar global category; demand exceeds supply (sold-out major festivals globally).
2. **Producer-as-celebrity.** Streaming-era stars (Fred again.., Peggy Gou, Charlotte de Witte, John Summit) have moved from underground to mainstream recognition without traditional TV exposure.
3. **Format fatigue in legacy reality.** Singing-competition formats are in measurable decline. Buyers need a new tentpole.
4. **Streaming/short-form distribution.** A music-discovery format now compounds via TikTok, Shorts, Reels — making in-show music releases a real business, not a vanity exercise.

Detailed evidence lives in `03_market_research/why_now.md`.

### The Format Hypothesis

MixHouse is not a singing show with DJs. It is a music-creation reality format with documentary-grade cinematic execution. Episode anatomy (`02_show_bible/episode_anatomy.md`) sets up:

- Contestants live and create together in a single residency location.
- Each episode produces a finished track that is released publicly the same week.
- Eliminations are driven by a combination of expert judging, live-audience reaction at curated club nights, and streaming performance of released tracks.
- The season ends with a headline festival slot for the winner — and a label deal that scales the IP across the year.

### The Franchise Hypothesis

MixHouse is engineered for multi-format expansion from season one:

- **Linear / streamer flagship** — primary cinematic season.
- **Music releases** — every episode produces a real, commercially released track.
- **Live touring** — finalists' tour anchored by season-end festival.
- **Brand partnerships** — beverage, fashion, tech, gaming integrations native to the scene.
- **International formats** — territory adaptations on the established `Idol`/`Voice` model.
- **Format extensions** — `MixHouse: Producers`, `MixHouse: Live`, junior, alumni reunion seasons.

See `05_business_model/franchise_flywheel.md`.

## Objections Answered

| Objection | Response |
| --- | --- |
| "Electronic music has been tried — *The Cube*, *Spin Off*, etc. — and failed." | Prior attempts were game-show / talent-only formats that did not borrow from cinematic documentary or reality character arcs. MixHouse's format engine combines all three. See `02_show_bible/format_engine.md`. |
| "The audience is too young / niche for broadcast." | The audience is younger than legacy reality, but the format is built for streamer-first distribution where that demo is the prize, not the problem. |
| "Music releases inside an unscripted show is operationally complex." | True, but the music release pipeline is a competitive moat once built. See `06_production_strategy/music_release_pipeline.md`. |
| "Why not a documentary instead?" | Documentaries don't build franchises. The format engine is what compounds. |
