# Founder Thesis

## Purpose

This document captures the originating worldview, conviction, taste, and cultural insight behind MixHouse, in the founder's own voice. Sam Salony is the founder and originating vision holder. Everything downstream in this knowledge base, the public website, investor materials, advisor conversations, casting outreach, production materials, and future storytelling, ultimately ladders back to the beliefs Sam articulates here.

This is not a biography. It is not a resume. It is not marketing copy. It is a conviction document. It is the file every other file should be reducible back to when a question about the soul of MixHouse comes up.

## How To Use This Document

- **For Sam (the founder).** Answer the prompts below in your own voice. Long form is fine. Fragments are fine. The point is to capture the worldview, not to write polished copy. The KB will translate this into derivative artefacts; what matters here is honesty and specificity.
- **For collaborators.** Read it before writing anything that claims to speak for MixHouse. Do not paraphrase it into marketing language without Sam's review.
- **For agents.** Treat this file as canonical input. When a downstream artefact contradicts the founder thesis, the artefact is wrong, not this file.
- **For investors and advisors.** Excerpts may be surfaced under confidentiality controls. The full document is `confidentiality: confidential`. Public-facing variants are produced separately, never by copy-paste from here.

A note on completion. This file is a template. Sam fills it in. The scaffolding below is prompts and structure, not assertions. Until Sam completes it, the file stays `assumption: true` and `status: draft`.

## What Existing Formats Get Wrong

> Specific failure modes Sam has seen, across the adjacent categories.

Prompts. For each category, name what feels outdated, what feels fake, what feels visually generic, what fails to create real artist outcomes, and what fails to respect the culture:

- **Reality TV.**
- **Music competition shows** (`Idol`, `Voice`, `X Factor`, `Rhythm + Flow`, `The Piano`, `American Song Contest`, prior DJ formats).
- **Prestige cinematic unscripted** (`Drive to Survive`, `Cheer`, `Quarterback`, `Welcome to Wrexham`).
- **Creator culture** (the Twitch / YouTube / TikTok-native unscripted that is now coming into TV).
- **Festival media** (livestreams, festival recaps, official aftermovies).
- **Artist documentaries** (Avicii, Daft Punk, deadmau5, Beyoncé, Billie Eilish).
- **Social media music discovery** (TikTok-led discovery cycles, Reels, Shorts).

_Sam to fill, category by category._

## What MixHouse Should Feel Like

> The emotional and cultural texture, not the mechanic.

Prompts for Sam to describe, in her own language:

- **Atmosphere.** What is in the room? What does the camera linger on?
- **Tone.** Earnest, dry, intimate, ambitious? Where on the dial?
- **Visual world.** What does the colour, the light, the framing look like? What references nearby?
- **Energy.** What is the pace? When does it breathe? When does it accelerate?
- **Intimacy.** Where does the audience get close? What do they overhear?
- **Pressure.** Where does the format make the contestants squeeze? Where does it let them rest?
- **Ambition.** What is the show trying to be, at its best?
- **Emotional stakes.** What do contestants stand to gain and lose, beyond the prize?
- **What viewers should feel.** During an episode, between episodes, at the end of a season.
- **What artists should feel.** Before applying, during the show, after the show.
- **What the internet should feel around it.** What is the texture of the social conversation we want?

_Sam to fill._

## Founder Convictions

> 10 to 20 specific beliefs Sam holds about MixHouse, music, audiences, and culture. These become the spine of the brand's positions.

Format: short, declarative, specific. Avoid generic startup language.

Examples (clearly marked as examples, not as final copy, replace with Sam's own):

- _Example:_ Audiences want participation, not passive viewing. Comments, predictions, pre-saves, and tickets are part of the artefact.
- _Example:_ Scene credibility cannot be manufactured by marketing. It is earned by who is in the room and what the room consents to film.
- _Example:_ Electronic artists are closer to creator brands than to traditional musicians. Their relationship with audience, release cadence, and visual identity reflects that.
- _Example:_ The music has to be real, or the show does not matter. A polished show with throwaway music is the failure mode we are designed against.
- _Example:_ The visual world of music is part of the music. Cinematography is a music decision, not a TV decision.
