# Supporting Operators

## Purpose

A modular, optional credibility layer for non-founder contributors and collaborators. This file exists to give investors, advisors, and partners a clear read on the execution surface around MixHouse without diluting the founder thesis.

Explicit framing:

- This is **optional add-on credibility material**. It is not required for the master argument to stand.
- This is **not founder mythology**. The originating vision belongs in `01_master_context/founder_thesis.md`.
- **Sam Salony remains the founder and originating vision holder of MixHouse.** Supporting operators add execution credibility, taste, network, judgment, and strategic leverage. They do not co-author the vision.
- Outputs that draw on this file (gated investor materials, advisor decks, team appendices, partner one-pagers) should always position supporting operators as supporting, not coequal, unless a contributor's formal status is genuinely coequal and Sam has agreed to that framing in writing.

## Usage Rules

Binding, before any profile is added or surfaced:

1. **Never position supporting contributors as coequal to the founder** unless that coequal status is formally true and documented.
2. **Do not overstate roles.** "Advisor" is not "Co-founder." "Contributor" is not "Producer." Use the words that are actually true.
3. **Do not invent credentials.** If a credit, title, or affiliation is not verifiable, do not include it. Route uncertainty to Open Questions.
4. **Distinguish confirmed from informal contributors.** Every profile carries an explicit Confirmed Status field. Informal advisors are not named in public-facing artefacts without consent.
5. **Keep bios short and strategic.** This is not LinkedIn. Each profile earns its space by answering a specific question about why this person makes MixHouse more likely to ship at quality.
6. **Focus on why each person matters specifically to MixHouse.** Generic career achievements without a MixHouse-specific value claim should be cut.
7. **Approval gate.** No contributor appears in any external-facing artefact (site, deck, pitch document) without explicit consent from the contributor and approval from Sam.

**Short Bio.** 2 to 4 sentences. Not LinkedIn style. Concrete, MixHouse-relevant, no filler.

**Operator Thesis.** One paragraph. Why this person matters specifically to MixHouse. Not a career summary. The specific contribution this person makes to the format's probability of working.

**Proof Points.** Bulleted, specific. Named projects, named outcomes, named relationships where appropriate and consented to. No vague endorsements.

**Usage Notes.** Where this profile should and should not be used. What context requires sign-off. What context is approved standing.

## Suggested Contributor Categories

Use these categories to group profiles as the file grows. Profiles can sit under more than one if genuinely true, but lead with the primary category.

- **Founder.** Sam Salony. The profile here is a short executive summary that links out to `01_master_context/founder_thesis.md` for the substantive document. Do not duplicate the founder thesis here.
- **Creative Strategy.** Concept development, format intuition, taste curation for the show itself.
- **Brand / Cultural Positioning.** Brand world, cultural posture, scene authenticity governance.
- **Interactive Experience.** Website, app, gated investor surfaces, audience-facing digital products.
- **Production Strategy.** Showrunning, line production, cinematic capture, music supervision pipeline.
- **Music Industry.** Label relationships, A&R, rights and publishing strategy.
- **Festival / Live Events.** Anchor festival relationships, `Drop Night` venue programming, live tentpole delivery.
- **Distribution / Audience Growth.** DSP relationships, short-form distribution, retention design.
- **Advisory Network.** Scene-credible producers, judges, journalists, cultural advisors. Most of this lives in `08_website_strategy/page_briefs/advisors.md`; only operators who genuinely advise on running the company belong here.
- **Investor / Business Strategy.** Capital strategy, franchise economics, format-sale playbook.
