# One-Sentence Positioning

## Purpose

Hold the canonical one-line positioning of MixHouse and the small set of approved variants for specific audiences (investors, broadcasters, advisors, talent, fans). Anyone writing copy elsewhere — pitch decks, website, press releases — pulls from this file.

## Key Takeaways

- There is exactly one canonical positioning sentence. All other lines are sanctioned variants.
- Variants exist to optimise for audience, never to reposition the franchise.
- Updating the canonical line requires owner sign-off and a Change Log entry.

## Core Content

### Canonical Positioning (working draft)

> **MixHouse is a cinematic reality franchise where the world's most exciting electronic-music producers live, create, and compete to release the records that will define a season — and a generation.**

This sentence holds:
- **Cinematic reality** — the production grammar.
- **Franchise** — the commercial frame.
- **Electronic-music producers** — the genre + talent specificity.
- **Live, create, compete** — the format engine.
- **Release the records** — the music-as-product loop.
- **Define a season — and a generation** — the cultural ambition.

### Approved Variants

| Audience | Variant | When to use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Investor** | "MixHouse is a multi-format franchise IP combining cinematic reality, real music releases, and live-event tentpoles — engineered to compound across linear, streaming, music, and live revenue." | Memos, decks, top of cap-table conversations. |
| **Broadcaster / streamer** | "MixHouse is the first prestige-grade unscripted franchise built for electronic music — character-led, cinematic, and franchisable across territories like *Idol* or *The Voice*." | Buy-side pitches. |
| **Advisor / industry** | "MixHouse turns the producer-as-celebrity moment into a franchise: a residency-based competition where the music released each week is the season's product." | Advisor and industry conversations. |
| **Talent (DJ/producer)** | "MixHouse is where the next generation of electronic producers go from underground to global — with real releases, real audiences, and real stakes." | Casting outreach. |
| **Fan / public site** | "Live in. Create together. Headline the world." | Website hero, social, trailer end-card. (See `08_website_strategy/page_briefs/home.md`.) |

### What Positioning is NOT

- Not "a DJ competition show". (Reduces ambition; misses production model.)
- Not "Love Island with DJs". (Useful shorthand internally; never in external copy.)
- Not a singing show, a talent search, or a documentary series.
- Not genre-agnostic. Electronic music is the franchise's anchor, not a category we visit.

### Words and Phrases That Stay Out

See full list in `00_system/research_prompt_template.md`. Specifically prohibited in the positioning line: "revolutionary", "next generation of music TV", "the future of reality", "where music meets reality", "passion for music".
