# Electronic Music Market

## Purpose

Size the global electronic-music market, recorded, live, sync, creator economy, at a level adequate to underwrite the franchise's commercial assumptions and answer "why electronic" questions in investor and broadcaster conversations.

## Key Takeaways

- Electronic music is one of the top three global music genres by listening and live attendance.
- Recorded-music share has grown steadily through the streaming era as DSPs surface electronic catalogues at scale.
- The live electronic-music sub-segment is the largest revenue concentration; festivals dominate.
- Creator-economy participation in electronic (producers, DJs, label runners) is high relative to mainstream genres.
- All numbers below are placeholders pending A-tier sourcing; this file is `assumption: true`.

### Geographic Skew

- Europe and Latin America index high on electronic-music live attendance.
- US and UK lead streaming consumption of electronic catalogues.
- APAC (especially India and Indonesia) is the fastest-growing live market for electronic.

This shapes:
- Where season 1 lives (Europe likely; see `06_production_strategy/location_strategy.md`).
- Where international format-sales target territories sit.

### Demographic Skew

- 18–34 over-indexed.
- Gender balance trending toward parity vs other genres.
- Higher disposable income per attendee on live electronic vs general music averages.

### Sub-Genre Composition

Electronic music is not one genre. The format must accommodate at least:
- House (deep / tech / progressive)
- Techno (peak time / hypnotic / industrial)
- Drum and bass / jungle
- UK garage / bassline
- Trance / progressive
- Hardstyle / hard dance
- Ambient / downtempo
- Dubstep / future bass
- Afro house / amapiano
- Hyperpop / electronic crossover

Sub-genre representation is a casting and judging axis (see `02_show_bible/contestant_archetypes.md`).

### What the Numbers Need to Prove

For the format to be commercially defensible, the data must support that:
1. Electronic catalogue can sustain a 60+ track season soundtrack at credible streaming volumes.
2. Live electronic events can host a finale at meaningful scale.
3. The 18–34 demo for streamers materially over-indexes on electronic.
4. Producer/DJ supply is deep enough to cast multiple seasons and international adaptations.
