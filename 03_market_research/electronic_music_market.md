# Electronic Music Market

## Purpose

Size the global electronic-music market across recorded, live, sync, and creator-economy segments, and document the demographic and geographic shape of its audience, at a level adequate to underwrite the franchise's commercial assumptions and answer "why electronic" questions in investor, broadcaster, and sponsor conversations.

This file is the single owner for **market sizing** and **electronic-music demographics** in the KB. Festival-specific sizing and live-event partner landscape live in `03_market_research/festival_live_experience_market.md`; producer-supply specifics live in `03_market_research/creator_economy_signals.md`; short-form and discovery behaviour lives in `03_market_research/music_discovery_behavior.md`. This file cites those files for depth and is cited by them for headline numbers.

## Key Takeaways

- Electronic music is a top-three global music genre by listening and live attendance, with the live segment indexing above its share of recorded.
- Audience demographics skew 18 to 34, with a more balanced gender mix than mainstream pop or hip-hop and above-median disposable income on live spend.
- Geographic concentration is real (Europe and Latin America for live; UK and US for streaming) but APAC growth (India, Indonesia, Korea, Japan) is the fastest leading indicator for territory adaptation.
- The headline numbers MixHouse cites must include: global recorded music revenue, electronic share of recorded, global live music revenue, electronic share of live, festival GMV concentration, and addressable producer population. Each row currently labelled `(target)` is a commitment to source, not a citation.
- Every quantitative claim in this file is currently `directional interpretation` pending A or B tier sourcing. The structure of the argument is binding; the calibration is not.

### Working Hypotheses (Directional Pending Sourcing)

The figures below are working assumptions. They are flagged as `directional interpretation` until replaced with sourced values. They are included so the argument can be stress-tested at structural level even before calibration.

- Global live music revenue is the largest single line in the music industry and has recovered above pre-pandemic levels (pending PwC / Live Nation source).

Sourcing these five replaces "directional interpretation" with "sourced fact" or, where the source disagrees with the working assumption, with a revised hypothesis.

### Sub-Genre Composition

Electronic is not one genre. The format must accommodate, and the casting funnel must source from, at least:

- House (deep, tech, progressive, soulful, micro-house)
- Techno (peak time, hypnotic, melodic, industrial)
- Drum and bass / jungle
- UK garage / bassline / speed garage
- Trance / progressive trance
- Hardstyle / hard dance / gabber
- Ambient / downtempo / lo-fi
- Dubstep / future bass / brostep
- Afro house / amapiano / 3-step
- Hyperpop / electronic crossover / digicore

Sub-genre representation is a casting axis and a judging axis (see `02_show_bible/contestant_archetypes.md` and `02_show_bible/judging_framework.md`).

### What The Numbers Need To Prove

For the format to be commercially defensible at investor and commissioning conversations, the data must support that:

1. Electronic catalogue can sustain a 60-plus track season soundtrack at credible streaming volumes (pending Chartmetric volume benchmarks).
2. Live electronic events can host a finale at meaningful scale (pending top-100 festival capacity data).
3. The 18 to 34 demo for streamers materially over-indexes on electronic (pending MIDiA / Luminate demographic cuts).
4. Producer and DJ supply is deep enough to cast multiple seasons and international adaptations (pending creator-economy data; cross-link `03_market_research/creator_economy_signals.md`).
5. Sponsor categories that index on this demo will pay premium for category-exclusive integration (pending IAB / WARC sponsorship reports; cross-link `05_business_model/sponsorship_inventory.md`).

## Objections Answered

| Objection | Response |
| --- | --- |
| "Electronic is a Europe story." | The geographic table acknowledges Western European concentration and flags North American streaming dominance and APAC and LatAm growth. The format-sale strategy is multi-region from the master thesis. |
| "Sub-genres fragment the audience." | The format spans sub-genres deliberately. Each cycle's resident judge panel represents different sub-genres. Casting policy enforces sub-genre coverage. |
| "All your numbers are placeholders." | Honest. The Evidence rows commit to specific sources at A and B tier. Until they land, the file stays `assumption: true` and downstream artefacts label numbers as illustrative. |
| "Why MixHouse not a pop or rap competition?" | Pop and rap recorded shares are larger than electronic, but live concentration, production-cycle compatibility (a week to finish a track), and creator-economy depth favour electronic. See `01_master_context/mixhouse_master_thesis.md` (Why Electronic Music). |
| "Demo data alone does not sell a format." | Correct. Demo is one of five inputs that sell the format; the others are the franchise mechanics, the cultural posture, the cinematic execution, and the release pipeline. Demo is necessary, not sufficient. |
