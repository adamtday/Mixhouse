# Reality TV Landscape

## Purpose

Map the current state of unscripted and reality formats, performance trends, commissioning behaviour, and the prestige-unscripted slot MixHouse targets, so the format positions itself accurately against the buyers and audiences that exist today.

This file is the single owner for **streamer unscripted trends** and **music-format fatigue evidence** in the KB. Per-comp deep dives live in `04_competitive_landscape/`; the editorial-and-economics analysis of how the prestige unscripted category evolved lives in `04_competitive_landscape/prestige_unscripted_breakdown.md`; this file is the market-shape and commissioning-signal owner.

## Key Takeaways

- The unscripted market has bifurcated. Legacy competition formats are in measurable ratings decline; prestige cinematic unscripted is a growing streamer commissioning category at scripted-grade cost-per-hour.
- Music-led formats are conspicuously absent from the prestige tier. Every adjacent category (sport, dance, dating, business) has at least one tentpole; music has none.
- Streamer commissioning prioritises 18 to 34 demos and franchise-able IP. Both line up with what MixHouse delivers.
- Singing-competition ratings decline is multi-year and structural, not a one-season slump. Trade press has tracked it across `Idol`, `Voice`, `X Factor`, and `The Masked Singer` franchises.
- All quantitative claims below are placeholders pending A or B tier sourcing. Specific CPH benchmarks, demo-share figures, and ratings deltas all require named-source citations before they leave this file.

## Core Content

### Market Structure (Four-Tier Model)

| Tier | Examples | Typical home | Trend |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Legacy competition** | `The Voice`, `Idol`, `X Factor`, `The Masked Singer` | Broadcast networks (NBC, FOX, ITV, BBC, RTL) | Declining ratings; format saturation; live-vote engagement at multi-year lows. |
| **Reality dating / lifestyle** | `Love Island`, `Selling Sunset`, `Below Deck`, `Vanderpump Rules` | Broadcast plus streamers | Stable for franchise leaders; pressure on imitators; tier benefits from short-form-clip economy. |
| **Prestige cinematic unscripted** | `Drive to Survive`, `The Last Dance`, `Cheer`, `Quarterback`, `Beckham`, `Welcome to Wrexham` | Streamers (Netflix, Apple TV+, ESPN, Disney+) | Rapidly growing commissioning category at scripted-grade CPH. |
| **Music documentary (one-off)** | Hulu / Apple / Netflix one-off artist docs (`Avicii: True Stories`, `Daft Punk Unchained`, `Renaissance: A Film by Beyoncé`) | Streamers | High demand, single-shot IP. Not franchise-able. |

MixHouse targets the prestige cinematic unscripted tier but as a **recurring format**, not a one-off. That combination is the open slot.

The category's decline is the structural backdrop for MixHouse's positioning. Trade press has covered the trend repeatedly; sourcing specific figures matters because investor and broadcaster conversations test it.

### The Prestige Unscripted "Slot Shape"

Patterns the prestige tier shares (per `04_competitive_landscape/prestige_unscripted_breakdown.md`):

1. Cinematic crews and scripted-grade DPs.
2. Character-led storytelling, not format-mechanic-led.
3. Real-world stakes (sport, performance, business, family).
4. Strong IP travel (territory adaptations, follow-up seasons, alumni spin-offs).
5. Sound design and music supervision at scripted level.

MixHouse satisfies every pattern and adds two not present in the prestige unscripted category today: a recurring competition mechanic and a real music release pipeline.

### What Music-Led Formats Have Tried

| Sub-category | What was tried | Outcome | Why it didn't fill the gap |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Singing competitions | `Idol`, `Voice`, `X Factor`, `Rhythm + Flow` | Franchise success in their tier; declining at the top | Cover-led or audition-led, not creation-led. No prestige cinematic identity. No real catalogue compounding. |
| Music documentary specials | Avicii, Daft Punk, Beyoncé, Billie Eilish, Taylor Swift | Critical and commercial success | High prestige but no franchise mechanic. Single-shot IP. |
| DJ talent shows (historical) | `Spin Off` (Fox 2014), `Master of the Mix` (Centric / VH1 2010-2013), `The Cube` (Hyundai branded 2014) | Cancelled or single-season | Competition-only. No cinematic layer. No character residency. No release pipeline. |
| Live performance docu-series | Cercle, Boiler Room, Apple Music's `Up Next` short films | Strong scene credibility; high view counts | No format engine. No competition stake. No character development. |
| Music-creation docu-series | `Songland` (NBC), `Watch the Sound with Mark Ronson` (Apple TV+) | Modest success, niche | Tightly scoped; no live-event payoff; no franchise rollout playbook. |

The combination MixHouse proposes (prestige cinematic + character residency + real music creation + live release pipeline) has not been credibly attempted at this tier. See `04_competitive_landscape/mixhouse_white_space.md`.

### Demo Economics

The 18 to 34 demo is over-represented in:

- Electronic-music listening and live attendance (see `03_market_research/electronic_music_market.md`).
- Streamer subscription churn risk (per multiple Netflix and Disney earnings cycles, specific data pending).
- Short-form video usage (per `03_market_research/music_discovery_behavior.md`).

A format that delivers this demo to a streamer is structurally valuable. Streamer commissioning prioritises this demo across the slate, and shareholders reward demo-aligned content because subscriber growth in this cohort has been the bottleneck for major streamers since 2023.

### Distribution Channels

- **Streamers.** Flagship home for season-one and territory tentpoles.
- **Linear broadcasters.** Territory deals; format adaptations; secondary windows.
- **Short-form platforms.** Derivative content; fan engagement; discovery; clipping.
- **Live events.** The finale and post-season touring as a distribution channel in their own right.
- **DSPs.** Music distribution; pre-save flows; editorial playlist support.

### Risks In This Landscape

| Risk | Mitigation |
| --- | --- |
| Streamer commissioning budgets are under pressure post-2023. | Multi-line revenue model means MixHouse is less dependent on streamer licence fees than competing formats. See `05_business_model/franchise_flywheel.md`. |
| New entrants (FAST channels, AVOD, YouTube originals) compete for unscripted IP. | These categories are downstream windowing opportunities, not competitive threats. |
| Long-tail catalogue is eating new-commission share in some markets. | Durable IP outlasts commissioning cycles. The music catalogue compounds even when the show is between seasons. |
| The prestige unscripted tier itself could compress if subscriber growth stalls. | The format's value to a streamer is demo-aligned subscriber retention, which is exactly what is under pressure. The pitch is more acute, not less. |

## Objections Answered

| Objection | Response |
| --- | --- |
| "Reality TV is a declining market." | Legacy reality is declining; prestige unscripted is growing. The mix shift, not the headline, matters. The four-tier table makes the bifurcation explicit. |
| "Music shows always die." | Singing-competition formats have. The prestige cinematic unscripted plus creation plus franchise combo has not been tested at this tier. The "What Music-Led Formats Have Tried" table maps the prior attempts to the structural reason each did not fill the gap. |
| "Streamers are pulling back." | They are reallocating, not pulling back, especially toward demo-aligned IP, which is exactly where MixHouse sits. |
| "CPH for prestige unscripted is too high to support a new franchise." | The format's multi-line revenue model and franchise-economics design make the CPH defensible. See `05_business_model/franchise_flywheel.md`. The streamer-by-streamer table identifies which buyers can underwrite the cost. |
| "The slot is too crowded already." | Music is absent from the slot. Every adjacent category has a tentpole; music has none. That is the operative gap. |
