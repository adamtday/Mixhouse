# Music Discovery Behaviour

## Purpose

Document how audiences, especially the 18 to 34 demo, discover, save, and convert into fans of new music in 2026, so MixHouse's release pipeline, short-form distribution, and fan-participation systems are designed against current behaviour, not 2015's.

This file is the single owner for **short-form discovery** behaviour and **DSP conversion mechanics** in the KB. Recorded-music market sizing lives in `03_market_research/electronic_music_market.md`; the operational release pipeline lives in `06_production_strategy/music_release_pipeline.md`; rights and label-partner strategy lives in `05_business_model/music_rights_strategy.md`.

## Key Takeaways

- Short-form video (TikTok, Reels, Shorts) is now the dominant music discovery channel for under-30 audiences. The trend is a five-plus-year structural shift, not a fad.
- Streaming behaviour follows discovery with a measurable lag (24 to 72 hours). The format's release cadence is built against this lag, not in spite of it.
- "Save to playlist / library" is a stronger conversion signal than play counts in most DSP algorithms. Pre-saves drive day-zero performance.
- Live attendance is a leading indicator of long-term fandom in electronic music, often preceding consistent streaming behaviour, the inverse of mainstream pop.
- Electronic music is structurally clip-able (percussive intros, recognisable drops, instrumental hooks) and the format is designed to produce short-form-native moments by mechanism, not retrofit.
- All behavioural claims below need A or B tier sourcing. The trend direction is robust across trade press; the specific magnitudes are still target rows.

## Core Content

### The Modern Discovery Funnel

| Stage | Channel | Signal | Cadence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Trigger** | Short-form clip, on-screen moment, sync, recommendation feed, friend share. | Saw / heard a clip. | Seconds. |
| **Save** | DSP "Add to Library / Playlist". | Intent to relisten. | Minutes to hours after trigger. |
| **Replay** | Algorithmic and intentional replays in the first 7 days. | Habituation. | Days. |
| **Live** | First gig or event attended (especially electronic). | Cementing fandom. | Weeks to months. |
| **Advocate** | Sharing, gifting, attending repeatedly, sound-using in own creator content. | Long-tail value. | Months to years. |

MixHouse is designed to compress this funnel inside a single season. The `Drop Night` moment plus immediate release plus short-form clip handles trigger and save inside the same week.

### Why Short-Form Matters Disproportionately For Electronic

Strategic hypothesis, with the structural reasons made explicit:

- **Electronic music is naturally clip-able.** Percussive intros, recognisable drops, instrumental hooks, sub-bass moments. A 15-second clip carries a complete musical unit. Pop and country need lyric context; electronic does not.
- **Short-form audiences over-index on under-30**, which over-indexes on electronic. The platform demographic and the genre demographic align.
- **Producer-as-celebrity status is now built on short-form first, releases second.** See `03_market_research/creator_economy_signals.md` for the named-case evidence.
- **The clip-to-stream attribution loop is shorter for electronic.** A clip leads to a DSP search, save, and replay faster than for genres where the track requires context. Specific attribution data pending Chartmetric / DSP partner research.
- **DJ sets recycle catalogue.** Tracks that lived in clips earlier in the year keep appearing in DJ sets months later, extending the streaming long tail well beyond the typical pop release cycle.

### Platform-By-Platform Behaviour

| Platform | Music role | What works | What MixHouse must produce |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **TikTok** | Primary discovery for under-25 globally. Sound-led culture; trending sounds drive search and save. | 15 to 60 second clips with a clean drop or hook. Sound use by other creators amplifies. | `Drop Night` clip per cycle; mentor / producer reaction moments; cast TikTok presence with sound credit. |
| **Instagram Reels** | Strong discovery for 18 to 34 demo; fashion / lifestyle adjacency. | Polished visuals; aesthetic-led; cinematic moments. | Cinematic-prestige clip cuts; backstage-aesthetic moments; brand-adjacent imagery. |
| **YouTube Shorts** | Growing music discovery channel; benefits from YouTube's catalogue and long-form integration. | Music-led clips with link-through to full audio or long-form on the same channel. | Same clip set as TikTok plus long-form (episode segments, Cercle-style cinematic sets). |
| **YouTube (long-form)** | Cinematic-live capture native home. Cercle, Boiler Room, festival livestreams all live here. | High production value, location-led, music-respecting. | `Drop Night` long-form cuts; mentor masterclass content; cinematic-prestige set captures. |
| **DSP editorial (Spotify, Apple Music, Amazon)** | Discovery-via-curation; relationship-led, not algorithmic. | Editorial relationship with electronic genre teams. | Pre-save flows; editorial submission; relationship management. See `05_business_model/music_rights_strategy.md`. |
| **DSP algorithmic (Spotify Discover Weekly, Apple Music personal stations)** | Discovery-via-algorithm; engagement-velocity-led. | First-72-hour save rate; full-play rate; skip rate. | Quality-controlled release pipeline; mentor sign-off gates; cohort-normalised distribution. |
| **Beatport / Traxsource** | Scene-credible DJ discovery; download-led. | Genre-specific charting; DJ-pool relationships. | Sub-genre-specific releases under scene-credible label partners. |
| **Resident Advisor / DJ Mag / Mixmag editorial** | Scene press; credibility tier. | Editorial coverage of scene moments; mix submissions. | Earned coverage via cultural authenticity posture; mix releases tied to season moments. |

### DSP Behaviour The Format Should Optimise For

- **First-72-hour save rate** matters more than first-hour play count.
- **Algorithmic playlist inclusion** depends on watch-through equivalents (full-play rate) and engagement velocity.
- **Pre-saves** drive day-zero performance and editorial-attention signal.
- **Editorial playlist support** is unlocked by relationship, not algorithm. See DSP partner strategy in `05_business_model/music_rights_strategy.md`.
- **Catalogue-position over time** matters for electronic in a way it does not for pop. A track that DJs continue to play three months later keeps re-entering Discover Weekly across the year.

### Short-Form Clip Inventory Per Cycle

Per `02_show_bible/format_engine.md`, each cycle produces:

| Clip type | Why it spreads | Distribution surface |
| --- | --- | --- |
| The drop moment | Cinematic single-take of the `Drop Night` peak; audio plus reaction is a complete short-form unit. | TikTok, Reels, Shorts. |
| The studio breakthrough | Producer's reaction the first time a finished track plays back; real, audio-anchored. | TikTok, Reels. |
| The mentor moment | Scene-credible artist working with a contestant; talent-fan amplification. | All platforms; brand-press potential. |
| The track itself | Released master with a clip-friendly drop or hook; independent of in-show footage. | TikTok sound page; DSP. |
| The elimination index reveal | Visualised composite-index reveal; structurally suspenseful. | TikTok, Reels, Shorts. |

The format produces five distinct clip types per cycle without anyone designing them after the fact. The clip pipeline is operationalised in `06_production_strategy/music_release_pipeline.md`.

### Live As A Leading Indicator

Counter-intuitive but important: in electronic music, going to a gig often precedes consistent streaming behaviour. A producer's live moment is what converts a curious listener into a regular streamer.

This justifies the format's investment in `Drop Night` and `Final Set`: they are not pageantry. They are the marketing engine of the discovery funnel.

Specific evidence required: IFPI consumer engagement reports; Pollstar attendee-to-streamer conversion studies; Spotify or Apple Music regional engagement cuts that show live attendance correlation with streaming behaviour.

### Behavioural Differences Vs Mainstream Pop

| Behaviour | Electronic | Mainstream pop |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Track discovery channel | Short-form plus DJ sets plus festival sets | Short-form plus radio plus curated DSP playlists |
| Save rate as fandom signal | Very strong | Moderate |
| Live attendance to conversion | Strong (often precedes streaming) | Weaker (follows streaming) |
| Long-tail catalogue listening | Strong (DJ sets revive old tracks) | Weaker |
| Artist-loyalty per release | Strong | Mixed |
| Clip-to-stream attribution speed | Faster | Slower |

The format is built around the electronic side of this table. Strategic hypothesis pending Luminate genre-by-genre behavioural cuts.

### Implications For The Format

- Release tracks on Friday post-`Drop Night` to align with weekend DSP listening peaks.
- Encourage pre-save flows the moment a track is confirmed for `Drop Night`.
- Build the `Crate` and curated playlists around real DSP behaviour patterns.
- Treat live attendance as a fan acquisition mechanic, not a finale stunt.
- Distribute clips across multiple short-form platforms with platform-native cuts, not single-source uploads.
- Build editorial DSP relationships in advance of season-one launch (long lead time on relationship-led editorial).

## Objections Answered

| Objection | Response |
| --- | --- |
| "Short-form is a fad." | It is now a five-plus-year structural channel and a billion-user behaviour. The format hedges by also driving direct DSP behaviour and live attendance. The "Modern Discovery Funnel" framing is multi-channel by design. |
| "DSPs control the funnel." | We work with DSP partners as strategic relationships, not as dependencies. See `05_business_model/music_rights_strategy.md`. The format generates demand-side signal (saves, pre-saves, full-plays) that DSPs reward; we are not asking for charity. |
| "Algorithms can be gamed; this is fragile signal." | Composite signal across four inputs (judges, crowd, streams, short-form) makes single-channel manipulation insufficient. Anomaly detection per `02_show_bible/elimination_logic.md`. |
| "Clip-driven discovery is for pop, not for prestige." | The prestige unscripted category itself is now distributed via short-form. `Drive to Survive` and `Cheer` both have substantial TikTok and Reels presences. Prestige and clip-native are not opposed. |
| "Editorial DSP support is unpredictable." | True. The mitigation is relationship-led, not algorithm-led: build the editorial relationship in advance of season-one launch and treat editorial as a partner, not a recipient. |
